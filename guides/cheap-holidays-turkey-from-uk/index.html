Cheap Holidays To Turkey From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Turkey is where East meets West in a spectacular display of culture, landscape, and unbeatable value for money. For UK visitors, the current economic climate makes Turkey one of the most affordable destinations globally, where your pound stretches further than almost anywhere else in the Mediterranean.

From the surreal landscapes of Cappadocia to the bustling bazaars of Istanbul and the pristine beaches of the Aegean coast, Turkey offers a depth of experience that belies its low cost. If you're looking for sun and sand, check out our budget guide to Antalya. By utilizing the extensive and modern bus network and embracing the local tea culture, you can explore this vast country on a remarkably modest budget. For those who want a stress-free experience, we have a dedicated all-inclusive Turkey guide.

Cheapest Time To Visit Turkey

The cheapest months to visit Turkey from the UK are:

  • April to early June
  • September to November

These months offer lower hotel prices and cheaper flights compared to peak summer.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £60–£180 return
  • Hotels: £20–£50 per night
  • All-inclusive packages: £300–£500

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Use the 'Havaist' or 'IETT' buses to get from Istanbul airports; they are clean, frequent, and much cheaper than taxis.
  • Download the 'Museum Pass Turkey' if you plan on visiting multiple historic sites like Ephesus and Hierapolis; it offers great savings.
  • Turkish tea (çay) is the social lubricant of the country; it's very cheap and often offered as a gesture of hospitality.
  • Learn a few basic Turkish phrases; locals highly appreciate the effort and it can often lead to better deals in the markets.

Best Areas to Stay in Turkey for Budget Travellers

In **Istanbul**, stay in the **Kadıköy** district on the Asian side for a more local, affordable vibe with incredible food. For beach lovers, **Fethiye** offers a great balance of beauty and budget, serving as a hub for the stunning Blue Lagoon. **Selçuk** is an excellent, low-cost base for exploring the ruins of Ephesus and the surrounding countryside.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Turkey

Expect to spend **£35 to £65 per day**. This budget is very generous in Turkey, allowing for stays in charming boutique hotels, multiple daily treats like 'Pide' or 'Lahmacun', and entry to major archaeological sites. Domestic flights within Turkey are also surprisingly cheap, making it easy to see different regions on a budget. You might also find similar value in our guide to Egypt on a budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Turkey from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Turkey is typically during April to early June and September to November, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Turkey cost from the UK?

Flights: £60–£180 return. Hotels: £20–£50 per night. All-inclusive packages: £300–£500

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Turkey?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Turkey?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Turkey holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Turkey due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
