Cheap Holidays To Tunisia From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Tunisia has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. If you're seeking North African value, you might also like our budget guide to Morocco. Whether you are planning to explore the ancient ruins of Carthage, visit the historic El Jem amphitheatre, or discover the blue village of Sidi Bou Said, this location offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Tunisia provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on layers of Roman and Islamic history and Saharan dunes, you can uncover the true essence of Tunisia while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control.

Cheapest Time To Visit Tunisia

The cheapest months to visit Tunisia from the UK are:

  • October to April
  • May and June

Spring and autumn offer perfect weather for sightseeing without the intense heat of mid-summer.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £150–£300 return
  • Hotels: £25–£55 per night
  • All-inclusive packages: £350–£600

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • All-inclusive deals often represent the best value here
  • Use 'Louages' (shared taxis) for cheap transport between towns
  • Visit the local Medinas for affordable leather goods and spices
  • Explore free or low-cost sites like the amphitheatres and ruins

Best Areas to Stay in Tunisia for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Tunisia. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the capital Tunis, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Tunisia, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the resort hub of Hammamet or the coastal Sousse, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Tunisia that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Tunisia

Budget travellers visiting Tunisia can typically expect to spend between £30 and £50 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale.

Local meals in Tunisia are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore Tunisia extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Tunisia from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Tunisia is typically during October to April and May and June, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Tunisia cost from the UK?

Flights: £150–£300 return. Hotels: £25–£55 per night. All-inclusive packages: £350–£600

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Tunisia?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Tunisia?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Tunisia holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Tunisia due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
