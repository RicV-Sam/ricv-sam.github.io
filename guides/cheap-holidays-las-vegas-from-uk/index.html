Cheap Holidays To Las Vegas From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Exploring Las Vegas on a budget is not only possible but incredibly rewarding for savvy travellers from the UK. Whether your heart is set on witnessing the iconic Las Vegas Strip, exploring the depths of the historic Fremont Street, or experiencing the majestic Red Rock Canyon, Las Vegas offers more than just expensive casinos. If you're looking for other iconic US city breaks, you might also like budget travel in Los Angeles. The accessibility provided by a wide range of airline partners means that finding a deal to Las Vegas is often just a matter of timing and a bit of research.

Once you've touched down, Las Vegas reveals its true value through its affordable infrastructure and local charm. The availability of world-class entertainment and neon-lit luxury ensures that every day is filled with wonder, regardless of your daily spend. By engaging with the local culture and choosing to stay in traditional neighbourhoods, you'll gain a deeper appreciation for Las Vegas while enjoying a holiday that is as affordable as it is unforgettable.

Cheapest Time To Visit Las Vegas

The cheapest months to visit Las Vegas from the UK are:

  • January and February
  • July and August (very hot)
  • November

Midweek stays (Sunday to Thursday) are always significantly cheaper than weekends.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Heathrow
  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £400–£700 return
  • Hotels (Mid-range): £40–£100 per night
  • Buffet lunch: £20–£40

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Always stay midweek to avoid huge weekend price hikes
  • Watch out for 'Resort Fees' which are added to the daily hotel rate
  • Buy drinks at a CVS or Walgreens on the Strip rather than in the casinos
  • Join players' clubs for free slot play and dining discounts

Best Areas to Stay in Las Vegas for Budget Travellers

Finding the perfect base in Las Vegas doesn't have to break the bank if you know where to look. For the best deals, focus your search on up-and-coming neighbourhoods like the affordable North Strip, which offer a great selection of budget accommodation. These spots are typically just a short hop from the main tourist zones, providing the perfect balance between convenience and cost-effectiveness.

Furthermore, districts like historic Downtown/Fremont and the residential Henderson are renowned for their student populations and local markets, which translates to affordable beds and even cheaper food. By choosing to stay in these lively, authentic areas, you'll benefit from a more immersive experience in Las Vegas while keeping your accommodation costs remarkably low. It's an excellent strategy for anyone looking to see the best of Las Vegas on a strict budget.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Las Vegas

To enjoy Las Vegas without overspending, a daily budget of approximately £80 and £130 is usually sufficient for the average backpacker or budget tourist. This figure accounts for basic accommodation, local transport, and satisfying meals from local eateries. It's a great destination for those who want a rich cultural experience without the high price tags found in other global hotspots, provided you make smart choices about where you eat and how you move around. Transport costs in Las Vegas are often one of the lowest parts of a traveller's budget, thanks to an extensive network of affordable local options. Similarly, food costs can be kept low by visiting local markets and bakeries for breakfast and lunch. By allocating your funds wisely and prioritizing the sights that matter most to you, you'll find that Las Vegas offers incredible value for every pound spent.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Las Vegas from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Las Vegas is typically during January and February, July and August (very hot) and November, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Las Vegas cost from the UK?

Flights: £400–£700 return. Hotels (Mid-range): £40–£100 per night. Buffet lunch: £20–£40

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Las Vegas?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Las Vegas?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Las Vegas holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Las Vegas due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
