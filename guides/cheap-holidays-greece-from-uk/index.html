Cheap Holidays To Greece From The UK | Budget Travel Guide

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Exploring Greece on a budget is not only possible but incredibly rewarding for savvy travellers from the UK. Whether your heart is set on witnessing the majestic Acropolis of Athens, exploring the depths of the white-washed village of Oia, or experiencing the ancient theatre of Epidaurus, you'll find that this destination caters exceptionally well to the cost-conscious. The accessibility provided by a wide range of airline partners means that finding a deal to Greece is often just a matter of timing and a bit of research.

Once you've touched down, Greece reveals its true value through its affordable infrastructure and local charm. The availability of cradle of Western civilization and idyllic islands ensures that every day is filled with wonder, regardless of your daily spend. By engaging with the local culture and choosing to stay in traditional neighbourhoods, you'll gain a deeper appreciation for Greece while enjoying a holiday that is as affordable as it is unforgettable.

Cheapest Time To Visit Greece

The cheapest months to visit Greece from the UK are:

  • May to June
  • September to October

These shoulder season months offer much better value for hotels and flights while still providing excellent weather for exploring the islands.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Gatwick
  • London Stansted
  • Manchester
  • Birmingham

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £80–£220 return
  • Hotels: £50–£120 per night
  • Package holidays: £350–£650

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Book during the shoulder seasons to avoid peak summer prices
  • Look for flights into larger hubs like Athens or Heraklion
  • Use local ferries instead of domestic flights for island hopping
  • Eat at local tavernas away from the main tourist strips

Best Areas to Stay in Greece for Budget Travellers

Finding the perfect base in Greece doesn't have to break the bank if you know where to look. For the best deals, focus your search on up-and-coming neighbourhoods like the historic Plaka in Athens, which offer a great selection of budget accommodation. These spots are typically just a short hop from the main tourist zones, providing the perfect balance between convenience and cost-effectiveness.

Furthermore, districts like affordable stays in Crete and the diverse islands of Rhodes are renowned for their student populations and local markets, which translates to affordable beds and even cheaper food. By choosing to stay in these lively, authentic areas, you'll benefit from a more immersive experience in Greece while keeping your accommodation costs remarkably low. It's an excellent strategy for anyone looking to see the best of Greece on a strict budget.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Greece

To enjoy Greece without overspending, a daily budget of approximately £50 and £80 is usually sufficient for the average backpacker or budget tourist. This figure accounts for basic accommodation, local transport, and satisfying meals from local eateries. It's a great destination for those who want a rich cultural experience without the high price tags found in other global hotspots, provided you make smart choices about where you eat and how you move around.

Transport costs in Greece are often one of the lowest parts of a traveller's budget, thanks to an extensive network of affordable local options. Similarly, food costs can be kept low by visiting local markets and bakeries for breakfast and lunch. By allocating your funds wisely and prioritizing the sights that matter most to you, you'll find that Greece offers incredible value for every pound spent.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Greece from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Greece is typically during May to June and September to October, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Greece?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Greece holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Greece due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
