Cheap All Inclusive Holidays To Greece From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

For many UK holidaymakers, Greece represents the perfect blend of exotic allure and wallet-friendly practicality. From the moment you arrive to see the majestic Acropolis of Athens or wander around the white-washed village of Oia, you'll realize why this has become such a popular choice for those looking to maximize their travel budget. If you're exploring the islands, see our budget guide to Crete. With frequent flights and diverse package options departing from regional UK airports, the journey to Greece is both convenient and surprisingly economical.

What truly sets Greece apart is the wealth of low-cost activities and dining options available. You can spend your days visiting the ancient theatre of Epidaurus or simply soaking in the local atmosphere, knowing that your money goes much further here than in typical European capitals. By prioritizing cradle of Western civilization and idyllic islands and opting for authentic local experiences, you can craft a memorable itinerary that respects your financial boundaries without sacrificing any of the magic. For more Mediterranean value, check out our cheap holidays to Cyprus.

Cheapest Time To Visit Greece

The cheapest months to visit Greece from the UK are:

  • May and June
  • September and October

Avoiding the peak July and August heat and crowds will also save you a significant amount on your package price.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester
  • Birmingham
  • East Midlands

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £60–£180 return
  • All-inclusive packages: £400–£750
  • Local Gyro snack: £3.50

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Check what's included—some 'all-inclusive' deals also include water sports
  • Look for hotels that offer a 'dine-around' program to sample local tavernas
  • Travel in a group to unlock better rates on larger family rooms or suites
  • Fly with low-cost carriers and book your own transfers to save more

Best Areas to Stay in Greece for Budget Travellers

When it comes to finding affordable lodging in Greece, location is everything. Savvy travellers often bypass the expensive city centres in favour of districts like the historic Plaka in Athens, where you can find much better value for your money. These neighbourhoods usually boast excellent transport links, making it a breeze to commute to the main sights while enjoying a quieter, more local environment at night.

Areas such as affordable stays in Crete and the diverse islands of Rhodes are particularly well-suited for those watching their pennies. Here, the concentration of budget-friendly hostels and simple apartments is higher, and the local dining scene reflects the more modest prices of the residents. Staying in these parts of Greece allows you to stretch your budget further, giving you more freedom to spend on experiences and attractions rather than just a place to sleep.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Greece

While your total spend in Greece will vary based on your travel style, most budget-conscious visitors find that £50 and £80 a day is a realistic target. This budget allows for a clean and safe hostel bed, several local meals, and enough left over for public transport and one or two modest paid attractions. You can further optimize your spending by taking advantage of lunch specials and free museum days which are common in many parts of Greece.

The cost of food and transport in Greece is generally quite reasonable, particularly if you steer clear of the most tourist-heavy plazas. Local buses and trains offer a cost-effective way to get around, and street food vendors provide a delicious and cheap alternative to sit-down dining. By setting a daily limit and sticking to it, you can enjoy all the highlights of Greece without any financial stress during or after your trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Greece?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Greece?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Greece holidays.

Package holidays often offer better value to Greece due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
