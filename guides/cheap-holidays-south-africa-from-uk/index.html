Cheap Holidays To South Africa From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

South africa has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. If you're looking for more value-driven adventures, you might also like our budget guide to Morocco. Whether you are planning to explore the iconic Table Mountain, visit the historic Robben Island, or discover the scenic Garden Route, this location offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, South Africa provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on incredible wildlife safaris and world-class vineyards, you can uncover the true essence of South Africa while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control.

Cheapest Time To Visit South Africa

The cheapest months to visit South Africa from the UK are:

  • May to September

This is the South African winter. It's the best time for game viewing in the north and offers the lowest prices for accommodation in Cape Town.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Heathrow
  • London Gatwick

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £550–£900 return
  • Hotels/Guesthouses: £40–£90 per night
  • Bottle of good local wine: £5–£10

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • The exchange rate is your friend—luxury experiences are often very affordable
  • Use the 'MyCiTi' bus in Cape Town or Uber for safe and cheap transport
  • Look for 'SanParks' accommodation for the most affordable safari stays
  • Eat at local markets like the Neighbourgoods Market for great value food

Best Areas to Stay in South Africa for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in South Africa. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the vibrant Cape Town, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In South Africa, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the bustling Johannesburg or the coastal Durban, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of South Africa that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in South Africa

Budget travellers visiting South Africa can typically expect to spend between £50 and £80 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale.

Local meals in South Africa are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore South Africa extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit South Africa from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit South Africa is typically during May to September, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

Flights: £550–£900 return. Hotels/Guesthouses: £40–£90 per night. Bottle of good local wine: £5–£10

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to South Africa?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to South Africa?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for South Africa holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to South Africa due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
