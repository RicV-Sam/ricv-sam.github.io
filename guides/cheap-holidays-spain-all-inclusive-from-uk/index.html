Cheap All Inclusive Holidays To Spain From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

For many UK holidaymakers, Spain represents the perfect blend of exotic allure and wallet-friendly practicality. From the moment you arrive to see the historic Alhambra or wander around the modern Guggenheim Museum, you'll realize why this has become such a popular choice for those looking to maximize their travel budget. If you're looking for island deals, check out our budget guide to Mallorca. With frequent flights and diverse package options departing from regional UK airports, the journey to Spain is both convenient and surprisingly economical.

What truly sets Spain apart is the wealth of low-cost activities and dining options available. You can spend your days visiting the vibrant Plaza Mayor or simply soaking in the local atmosphere, knowing that your money goes much further here than in typical European capitals. By prioritizing tapas culture and diverse regional traditions and opting for authentic local experiences, you can craft a memorable itinerary that respects your financial boundaries without sacrificing any of the magic.

Cheapest Time To Visit Spain

The cheapest months to visit Spain from the UK are:

  • January to March
  • October to November

Spain is a year-round destination, and the winter months offer incredible bargains for those who don't mind slightly cooler temperatures.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Luton
  • Manchester
  • Liverpool
  • Birmingham
  • Glasgow

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £40–£120 return
  • All-inclusive packages: £350–£650
  • Beer (if not all-in): £2–£4

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Look for 'Last Minute' deals—Spain often has the best true last-minute bargains
  • Fly midweek to avoid the higher Friday and Saturday flight prices
  • Consider the Costa Almeria or Costa Calida for even better value than the main hubs
  • Use the excellent Spanish rail and bus network for cheap day trips

Best Areas to Stay in Spain for Budget Travellers

When it comes to finding affordable lodging in Spain, location is everything. Savvy travellers often bypass the expensive city centres in favour of districts like the cultural Madrid, where you can find much better value for your money. These neighbourhoods usually boast excellent transport links, making it a breeze to commute to the main sights while enjoying a quieter, more local environment at night.

Areas such as the artistic Barcelona and the sunny Costa del Sol are particularly well-suited for those watching their pennies. Here, the concentration of budget-friendly hostels and simple apartments is higher, and the local dining scene reflects the more modest prices of the residents. Staying in these parts of Spain allows you to stretch your budget further, giving you more freedom to spend on experiences and attractions rather than just a place to sleep.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Spain

While your total spend in Spain will vary based on your travel style, most budget-conscious visitors find that £50 and £80 a day is a realistic target. This budget allows for a clean and safe hostel bed, several local meals, and enough left over for public transport and one or two modest paid attractions. You can further optimize your spending by taking advantage of lunch specials and free museum days which are common in many parts of Spain.

The cost of food and transport in Spain is generally quite reasonable, particularly if you steer clear of the most tourist-heavy plazas. Local buses and trains offer a cost-effective way to get around, and street food vendors provide a delicious and cheap alternative to sit-down dining. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Spain from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Spain is typically during January to March and October to November, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Spain cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£120 return. All-inclusive packages: £350–£650. Beer (if not all-in): £2–£4

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Spain?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Spain?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Spain holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Spain due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
