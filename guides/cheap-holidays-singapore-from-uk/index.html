Cheap Holidays To Singapore From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Exploring Singapore on a budget is not only possible but incredibly rewarding for savvy travellers from the UK. Whether your heart is set on witnessing the futuristic Gardens by the Bay, exploring the depths of the iconic Marina Bay Sands, or experiencing the vibrant Jewel Changi Airport, you'll find that this destination caters exceptionally well to the cost-conscious. The accessibility provided by a wide range of airline partners means that finding a deal to Singapore is often just a matter of timing and a bit of research.

Once you've touched down, Singapore reveals its true value through its affordable infrastructure and local charm. The availability of unparalleled street food and garden city innovation ensures that every day is filled with wonder, regardless of your daily spend. If you're exploring Southeast Asia, you might also like budget travel in Thailand. By engaging with the local culture and choosing to stay in traditional neighbourhoods, you'll gain a deeper appreciation for Singapore while enjoying a holiday that is as affordable as it is unforgettable. Cheapest Time To Visit Singapore

The cheapest months to visit Singapore from the UK are:

  • August to October
  • January to March (excluding Chinese New Year)

Avoiding major festivals and events like the F1 Grand Prix is essential for budget travel.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Heathrow
  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £550–£900 return
  • Hotels/Hostels: £30–£80 per night
  • Hawker meal: £4–£8

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Eat at Hawker Centers—the food is world-class and very cheap
  • Use the MRT (Subway)—it's clean, efficient, and very affordable
  • Visit free attractions like the Botanic Gardens and the Merlion Park
  • Drink tap water—it's safe and will save you a fortune over a week

Best Areas to Stay in Singapore for Budget Travellers

Finding the perfect base in Singapore doesn't have to break the bank if you know where to look. For the best deals, focus your search on up-and-coming neighbourhoods like the cultural Little India, which offer a great selection of budget accommodation. These spots are typically just a short hop from the main tourist zones, providing the perfect balance between convenience and cost-effectiveness.

Furthermore, districts like vibrant Chinatown and the budget-friendly Geylang are renowned for their student populations and local markets, which translates to affordable beds and even cheaper food. By choosing to stay in these lively, authentic areas, you'll benefit from a more immersive experience in Singapore while keeping your accommodation costs remarkably low. It's an excellent strategy for anyone looking to see the best of Singapore on a strict budget.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Singapore

To enjoy Singapore without overspending, a daily budget of approximately £80 and £130 is usually sufficient for the average backpacker or budget tourist. This figure accounts for basic accommodation, local transport, and satisfying meals from local eateries. It's a great destination for those who want a rich cultural experience without the high price tags found in other global hotspots, provided you make smart choices about where you eat and how you move around.

Transport costs in Singapore are often one of the lowest parts of a traveller's budget, thanks to an extensive network of affordable local options. Similarly, food costs can be kept low by visiting local markets and bakeries for breakfast and lunch. By allocating your funds wisely and prioritizing the sights that matter most to you, you'll find that Singapore offers incredible value for every pound spent. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Singapore from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Singapore is typically during August to October and January to March (excluding Chinese New Year), when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Singapore cost from the UK?

Flights: £550–£900 return. Hotels/Hostels: £30–£80 per night. Hawker meal: £4–£8

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Singapore?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Singapore?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Singapore holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Singapore due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
