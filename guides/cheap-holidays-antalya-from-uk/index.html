Cheap Holidays To Antalya From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Antalya from the UK is the gateway to the Turkish Riviera, offering a stunning mix of ancient history, turquoise waters, and some of the best-value all-inclusive resorts in the Mediterranean. Whether you're planning to marvel at the Roman-era Hadrian's Gate or relax on the sun-soaked Konyaaltı Beach, Antalya provides a rich cultural experience that respects a modest budget. For UK travellers, the sheer volume of budget flights and competitive 2026 holiday packages makes it a top-tier value destination.

The secret to a cheap Antalya trip is balancing the resort life with the charm of the "Old Town" (Kaleiçi). Spend your days exploring Roman ruins or hiking to the cascading Düden Waterfalls for just a few Lira. If you are looking for more affordable beach breaks in the region, you might also like cheap holidays to Sharm El Sheikh. By staying in traditional boutique pensions in Kaleiçi and dining at local "Lokantas," you can experience authentic Turkish hospitality without needing a luxury budget. From the golden sands of Lara Beach to the historic ancient harbour, Antalya offers a depth of adventure that is as affordable as it is unforgettable. Those interested in more Turkish value should also see our overall budget guide to Turkey.

Cheapest Time To Visit Antalya

The cheapest months to visit Antalya are April, May, and October into November.

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £65–£175 return from major UK airports
  • Hotels: £35–£95 per night for a central boutique guesthouse or hotel
  • All-inclusive packages: £290–£540 for a 7-night sun break

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Eat at a 'Lokanta': These traditional Turkish tradesmen's restaurants serve delicious, home-cooked stews, pilafs, and kebabs at local prices. They are the best way to eat well on a budget.
  • Use the Antray Tram: Skip the expensive airport taxis. The modern Antray tram system is incredibly cheap and connects the airport directly to the historic center and Konyaaltı Beach.
  • Visit the Public Beaches: Lara and Konyaaltı both have large public sections that are free to use, offering the same stunning Mediterranean views as the expensive beach clubs.
  • Shop at the Weekly Bazaars: Avoid the tourist gift shops. Visit the weekly neighbourhood bazaars for fresh fruit, local olives, and authentic textiles at genuine local prices.

Best Areas to Stay in Antalya for Budget Travellers

To find the best value and atmosphere, consider these diverse neighbourhoods:

  • Kaleiçi (Old Town): The historic heart of Antalya is filled with beautifully restored Ottoman-era mansions that now serve as affordable boutique hotels and pensions.
  • Konyaaltı: Located to the west of the center, this area offers modern budget apartments and guesthouses within walking distance of the beach and the Antray tram line.
  • Lara (City Side): While the beachfront is lined with luxury resorts, the residential streets just behind Lara Beach offer excellent value-for-money hotels and local dining.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Antalya

Expect to spend **£40 to £75 per day**. This budget is very generous given the local prices, allowing for great meals of 'Piyaz' and 'Kebab', entry to major archaeological sites, and plenty of Turkish tea.
