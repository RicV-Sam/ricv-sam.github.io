Cheap Holidays To Vietnam From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

For many UK holidaymakers, Vietnam represents the perfect blend of exotic allure and wallet-friendly practicality. From the moment you arrive to see the stunning Ha Long Bay or wander around the historic Hoi An Ancient Town, you'll realize why this has become such a popular choice for those looking to maximize their travel budget. With frequent flights and diverse package options departing from regional UK airports, the journey to Vietnam is both convenient and surprisingly economical.

What truly sets Vietnam apart is the wealth of low-cost activities and dining options available. You can spend your days visiting the vibrant Cu Chi Tunnels or simply soaking in the local atmosphere, knowing that your money goes much further here than in typical European capitals. If you're exploring the region, you might also consider visiting Cambodia on a budget. By prioritizing exceptional coffee culture and remarkable natural beauty and opting for authentic local experiences, you can craft a memorable itinerary that respects your financial boundaries without sacrificing any of the magic. Cheapest Time To Visit Vietnam

The cheapest months to visit Vietnam from the UK are:

  • May and June
  • September and October

The weather varies by region, but the shoulder seasons generally offer the best value across the whole country.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Heathrow
  • London Gatwick

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £550–£850 return
  • Hotels: £15–£40 per night
  • Banh Mi (sandwich): £1–£2

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Eat street food—it's the best way to experience Vietnam's cuisine cheaply
  • Use the 'Grab' app for affordable and safe taxi/scooter rides
  • Take the 'Reunification Express' train for scenic and cheap long-distance travel
  • Drink 'Bia Hoi' (fresh beer) for under 50p in many cities

Best Areas to Stay in Vietnam for Budget Travellers

When it comes to finding affordable lodging in Vietnam, location is everything. Savvy travellers often bypass the expensive city centres in favour of districts like the cultural Hanoi, where you can find much better value for your money. These neighbourhoods usually boast excellent transport links, making it a breeze to commute to the main sights while enjoying a quieter, more local environment at night.

Areas such as vibrant Ho Chi Minh City and the historic Hoi An are particularly well-suited for those watching their pennies. Here, the concentration of budget-friendly hostels and simple apartments is higher, and the local dining scene reflects the more modest prices of the residents. Staying in these parts of Vietnam allows you to stretch your budget further, giving you more freedom to spend on experiences and attractions rather than just a place to sleep.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Vietnam

While your total spend in Vietnam will vary based on your travel style, most budget-conscious visitors find that £30 and £50 a day is a realistic target. This budget allows for a clean and safe hostel bed, several local meals, and enough left over for public transport and one or two modest paid attractions. You can further optimize your spending by taking advantage of lunch specials and free museum days which are common in many parts of Vietnam. The cost of food and transport in Vietnam is generally quite reasonable, particularly if you steer clear of the most tourist-heavy plazas. Local buses and trains offer a cost-effective way to get around, and street food vendors provide a delicious and cheap alternative to sit-down dining. By setting a daily limit and sticking to it, you can enjoy all the highlights of Vietnam without any financial stress during or after your trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Vietnam from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Vietnam is typically during May and June and September and October, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Vietnam cost from the UK?

Flights: £550–£850 return. Hotels: £15–£40 per night. Banh Mi (sandwich): £1–£2

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Vietnam?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Vietnam?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Vietnam holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Vietnam due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
