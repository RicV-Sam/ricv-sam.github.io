Cheap Holidays To Bruges From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Bruges has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. Whether you are planning to explore the medieval Belfry tower, visit the picturesque Lake of Love, or discover the historic Markt square, this location offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Bruges provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on fairytale canals and world-renowned Belgian chocolate, you can uncover the true essence of Bruges while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control.

Cheapest Time To Visit Bruges

January, February, and November are the most affordable months to visit Bruges.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Heathrow
  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester
  • Edinburgh
  • Birmingham

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £40–£130 return
  • Hotels: £80–£180 per night
  • City break packages: £220–£450

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Climb the Belfry early in the morning for the best views
  • Buy chocolate from local supermarkets for a fraction of the boutique price
  • Walk the canal paths away from the main square
  • Eat from the local markets for a cheap lunch

Best Areas to Stay in Bruges for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Bruges. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the Sint-Anna Quarter, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Bruges, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the quiet Sint-Gillis or the central Historic Centre, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Bruges that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Bruges

Budget travellers visiting Bruges can typically expect to spend between £50 and £80 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale.

Local meals in Bruges are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore Bruges extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Bruges from the UK?

The winter months of January and February offer the best value for a Bruges city break.

How much does a holiday to Bruges cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£130 return. Hotels: £80–£180 per night. City break packages: £220–£450

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Bruges?

Most travellers fly into Brussels (BRU) or Charleroi (CRL) and take the easy train to Bruges.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Bruges?

Booking 2 months in advance is ideal for finding affordable and central accommodation.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

DIY booking is often preferred for Bruges to allow for more flexible travel options.
