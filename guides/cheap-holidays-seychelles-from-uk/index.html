Cheap Holidays To Seychelles From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

The seychelles has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. If you're looking for more island value, check out our budget guide to Mauritius. Whether you are planning to explore the iconic Anse Source d'Argent, visit the lush Vallée de Mai, or discover the pristine Curieuse Island, this location offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, the Seychelles provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on unique granite boulders and rare giant tortoises, you can uncover the true essence of the Seychelles while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control.

Cheapest Time To Visit Seychelles

The cheapest months to visit Seychelles from the UK are:

  • May to September

While this is the drier, cooler season, it's often the best time to find competitive rates on guesthouses and flights.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Heathrow

Most flights have a stopover in the Middle East (Dubai/Doha/Abu Dhabi).

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £650–£950 return
  • Guesthouses: £70–£130 per night
  • Takeaway meal box: £5–£10

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Eat at 'Takeaway' stands—they offer delicious Creole meals for very little money
  • Use the public buses on Mahé and Praslin for just a few pence per ride
  • Rent a bicycle on La Digue—it's the main way to get around and very cheap
  • Buy your groceries at local markets and shops to save on dining out

Best Areas to Stay in the Seychelles for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in the Seychelles. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the main island of Mahé, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In the Seychelles, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the tranquil Praslin or the laid-back La Digue, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of the Seychelles that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in the Seychelles

Budget travellers visiting the Seychelles can typically expect to spend between £80 and £130 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale.

Local meals in the Seychelles are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore the Seychelles extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Seychelles from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Seychelles is typically during May to September, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Seychelles cost from the UK?

Flights: £650–£950 return. Guesthouses: £70–£130 per night. Takeaway meal box: £5–£10

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Seychelles?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Seychelles?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Seychelles holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Seychelles due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
