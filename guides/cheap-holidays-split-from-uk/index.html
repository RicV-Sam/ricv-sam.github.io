Cheap Holidays To Split From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Exploring Split on a budget is not only possible but incredibly rewarding for savvy travellers from the UK. Whether your heart is set on witnessing the historic Diocletian's Palace, exploring the depths of the scenic Riva promenade, or experiencing the lush Marjan Hill, you'll find that this destination caters exceptionally well to the cost-conscious. The accessibility provided by a wide range of airline partners means that finding a deal to Split is often just a matter of timing and a bit of research.

Once you've touched down, Split reveals its true value through its affordable infrastructure and local charm. The availability of ancient Roman living and gateway to the islands ensures that every day is filled with wonder, regardless of your daily spend. By engaging with the local culture and choosing to stay in traditional neighbourhoods, you'll gain a deeper appreciation for Split while enjoying a holiday that is as affordable as it is unforgettable.

Cheapest Time To Visit Split

May, September, and October are the best months for a cheap holiday in Split.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Gatwick
  • London Luton
  • Manchester
  • Bristol
  • Glasgow

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £70–£180 return
  • Hotels: £60–£130 per night
  • Holiday packages: £350–£600

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Use the local Jadrolinija ferries for affordable island hopping
  • Eat 'Burek' from local bakeries for a cheap and filling snack
  • Hike up Marjan Hill for free panoramic views of the city
  • Visit the local green market (Pazar) for fresh produce

Best Areas to Stay in Split for Budget Travellers

Finding the perfect base in Split doesn't have to break the bank if you know where to look. For the best deals, focus your search on up-and-coming neighbourhoods like the historic Old Town, which offer a great selection of budget accommodation. These spots are typically just a short hop from the main tourist zones, providing the perfect balance between convenience and cost-effectiveness.

Furthermore, districts like the social Bačvice and the authentic Varoš are renowned for their student populations and local markets, which translates to affordable beds and even cheaper food. By choosing to stay in these lively, authentic areas, you'll benefit from a more immersive experience in Split while keeping your accommodation costs remarkably low. It's an excellent strategy for anyone looking to see the best of Split on a strict budget.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Split

To enjoy Split without overspending, a daily budget of approximately £50 and £80 is usually sufficient for the average backpacker or budget tourist. This figure accounts for basic accommodation, local transport, and satisfying meals from local eateries. It's a great destination for those who want a rich cultural experience without the high price tags found in other global hotspots, provided you make smart choices about where you eat and how you move around.

Transport costs in Split are often one of the lowest parts of a traveller's budget, thanks to an extensive network of affordable local options. Similarly, food costs can be kept low by visiting local markets and bakeries for breakfast and lunch. By allocating your funds wisely and prioritizing the sights that matter most to you, you'll find that Split offers incredible value for every pound spent.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Split from the UK?

May and October offer great weather and much lower prices than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Split cost from the UK?

Flights: £70–£180 return. Hotels: £60–£130 per night. Holiday packages: £350–£600

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Split?

Split Airport (SPU) is well-connected to major UK cities by several low-cost airlines.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Split?

Booking 3–4 months in advance is recommended to secure the best rates during the shoulder season.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

A mix of DIY for flexibility and packages for resorts can both offer good value in Split.
