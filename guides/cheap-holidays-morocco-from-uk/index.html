Cheap Holidays To Morocco From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Morocco has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. Whether you are planning to explore the Sahara Desert dunes, visit the blue city of Chefchaouen, or discover the historic Fes Medina, this location offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Morocco provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. If you're looking for more city break inspiration in the region, you might also like budget travel in Marrakech. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on extraordinary sensory experiences and desert landscapes, you can uncover the true essence of Morocco while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control. Cheapest Time To Visit Morocco

The cheapest months to visit Morocco from the UK are:

  • November to February
  • May and June

Winter is mild and perfect for exploring cities, while the coast remains pleasant year-round.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester
  • Bristol

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £40–£130 return
  • Riads: £30–£60 per night
  • Street food Tagine: £4–£7

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Stay in a Riad inside the Medina for better prices and atmosphere
  • Drink mint tea—it's delicious, traditional, and very cheap
  • Haggling is expected in markets; never pay the first price
  • Use the CTM or Supratours buses for reliable and cheap inter-city travel

Best Areas to Stay in Morocco for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Morocco. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as vibrant Marrakech, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Morocco, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the coastal Agadir or the artistic Essaouira, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Morocco that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Morocco

Budget travellers visiting Morocco can typically expect to spend between £30 and £50 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale. Local meals in Morocco are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore Morocco extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Morocco from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Morocco is typically during November to February and May and June, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Morocco cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£130 return. Riads: £30–£60 per night. Street food Tagine: £4–£7

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Morocco?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Morocco?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Morocco holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Morocco due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
