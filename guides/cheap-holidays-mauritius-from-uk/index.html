Cheap Holidays To Mauritius From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Mauritius has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. If you're looking for other tropical value, you might also like budget travel in Bali. Whether you are planning to explore the unique Seven Coloured Earth, visit the iconic Le Morne Brabant, or discover the lush Pamplemousses Garden, Mauritius offers a remarkable balance of beauty and accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Mauritius provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on pristine coral reefs and diverse tropical forests, you can uncover the true essence of Mauritius while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control.

Cheapest Time To Visit Mauritius

The cheapest months to visit Mauritius from the UK are:

  • May to September

This is the Mauritian winter—the weather is still warm and sunny, but hotel prices are at their lowest and the island is less crowded.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Heathrow
  • London Gatwick

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £600–£950 return
  • Guesthouses/Apartments: £40–£90 per night
  • Street food 'Dholl Puri': £2

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Use the local bus network—it's cheap and covers the whole island
  • Eat local street food like 'Dholl Puri' and 'Gateaux Piment'
  • Stay in Flic en Flac or Grand Baie for the best range of budget options
  • All beaches are public—find your favorite spot and enjoy for free

Best Areas to Stay in Mauritius for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Mauritius. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the resort hub of Grand Baie, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Mauritius, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the scenic Flic en Flac or the capital Port Louis, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Mauritius that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Mauritius

Budget travellers visiting Mauritius can typically expect to spend between £80 and £130 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale. Local meals in Mauritius are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore Mauritius extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Mauritius?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Mauritius?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Mauritius holidays.

Package holidays often offer better value to Mauritius due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
