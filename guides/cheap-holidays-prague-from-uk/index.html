Cheap Holidays To Prague From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Exploring Prague on a budget is not only possible but incredibly rewarding for savvy travellers from the UK. Whether your heart is set on witnessing the historic Charles Bridge, exploring the depths of the sprawling Prague Castle, or experiencing the iconic Old Town Square, you'll find that this destination caters exceptionally well to the cost-conscious. The accessibility provided by a wide range of airline partners means that finding a deal to Prague is often just a matter of timing and a bit of research.

Once you've touched down, Prague reveals its true value through its affordable infrastructure and local charm. The availability of Gothic architecture and world-leading beer culture ensures that every day is filled with wonder, regardless of your daily spend. By engaging with the local culture and choosing to stay in traditional neighbourhoods, you'll gain a deeper appreciation for Prague while enjoying a holiday that is as affordable as it is unforgettable.

Cheapest Time To Visit Prague

The cheapest time to visit Prague is between January and March, or during the late autumn months of October and November.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Luton
  • Manchester
  • Edinburgh
  • Birmingham

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £40–£120 return
  • Hotels: £60–£140 per night
  • City break packages: £180–£350

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Avoid changing money on the street; use official banks or reputable offices like 'Exchange' near the Old Town Square.
  • The 24-hour or 72-hour transport passes are incredible value and work on all trams, buses, and the metro.
  • Eat at 'Lunchit' or similar local canteens for authentic, cheap meals.
  • Climb Petřín Hill for views instead of paying for the elevator.

Best Areas to Stay in Prague for Budget Travellers

While the Old Town is beautiful, stay in **Žižkov** or **Vinohrady** for the best value. Žižkov is known for having the highest density of pubs in Europe and very affordable prices, while Vinohrady offers a more upscale but still budget-friendly residential feel with great parks.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Prague

A very comfortable budget is **£50 to £90 per day**. You'll eat like a king on traditional goulash and dumplings, enjoy some of the world's best pilsners, and still have enough for museum entries or a river cruise. It's one of the few capitals where you can still find central hostels for under £20.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Prague from the UK?

January to March is the most affordable period, outside of the busy Christmas market season.

How much does a holiday to Prague cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£120 return. Hotels: £60–£140 per night. City break packages: £180–£350

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Prague?

Václav Havel Airport (PRG) is well-connected to the UK via low-cost carriers like Ryanair and Wizz Air.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Prague?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance is recommended to find the best value on central accommodation.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

DIY booking is often cheaper for Prague due to the high volume of low-cost flights and varied hotel options.
