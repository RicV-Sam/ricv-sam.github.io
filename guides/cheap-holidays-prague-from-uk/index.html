Cheap Holidays To Prague From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Prague from the UK is one of the most rewarding value destinations in Europe, where your pound goes significantly further than in most western capitals. Whether your heart is set on witnessing the historic Charles Bridge, exploring the sprawling Prague Castle, or experiencing the iconic Old Town Square, you'll find that the "City of a Hundred Spires" caters exceptionally well to the cost-conscious. For UK visitors, the abundance of low-cost flights makes it a prime candidate for a 2026 budget city break. The secret to an affordable Prague trip is stepping away from the immediate tourist hubs of Prague 1. Once you've touched down, the city reveals its true value through its world-leading beer culture and hearty, affordable cuisine. If you are looking for other affordable historic cities, you might also consider cheap holidays to Krakow, which offers a similarly rich atmosphere. By engaging with the local culture in neighbourhoods like Žižkov or Vinohrady, you'll gain a deeper appreciation for Prague while enjoying a holiday that is as culturally rich as it is unforgettable.

Cheapest Time To Visit Prague

The cheapest time to visit Prague is between January and March, or during the late autumn months of October and November.

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Drink the Local Beer: In Prague, local beer (Pivo) is often cheaper than bottled water or soft drinks. Look for pubs serving Pilsner Urquell or Budvar for the best value and quality.
  • Use the Public Transport: Prague's Metro, tram, and bus network is exceptional. A 24-hour or 72-hour pass is incredibly cheap and covers all transport, including the Petřín funicular.
  • Avoid the "Trdelník" Tourist Trap: While popular, these chimney cakes are expensive and not traditionally Czech. Instead, visit a local 'cukrárna' (pastry shop) for authentic and cheaper treats.
  • Eat a 'Polední Menu': On weekdays, most local restaurants offer a discounted 'noon menu' (fixed-price lunch) that includes soup and a main course for a fraction of the dinner price.

Best Areas to Stay in Prague for Budget Travellers

To find the best value accommodation and authentic local life, look beyond the immediate Old Town (Prague 1):

  • Žižkov (Prague 3): Historically a working-class district, it reportedly has the highest density of pubs in Europe and offers some of the city's best budget hotels and hostels.
  • Vinohrady (Prague 2): A beautiful residential area with leafy streets, great parks, and a fantastic range of affordable cafes and international dining.
  • Holešovice (Prague 7): An up-and-coming, trendy area with creative spaces, great value galleries, and affordable, cool guesthouses.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Prague from the UK?

January to March is the most affordable period, outside of the busy Christmas market season.

How much does a holiday to Prague cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£120 return. Hotels: £60–£140 per night. City break packages: £180–£350

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Prague?

Václav Havel Airport (PRG) is well-connected to the UK via low-cost carriers like Ryanair and Wizz Air.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Prague?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance is recommended to find the best value on central accommodation.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

DIY booking is often cheaper for Prague due to the high volume of low-cost flights and varied hotel options.
