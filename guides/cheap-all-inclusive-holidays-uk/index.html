Cheap All Inclusive Holidays From The UK

5 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

All inclusive holidays can offer excellent value for UK travellers, especially when food, drinks and entertainment are included in one upfront price. With the right booking strategy, all inclusive holidays can sometimes be cheaper than booking flights and hotels separately. Savvy UK holidaymakers are increasingly turning to these "locked-in" price packages as a way to protect against fluctuating local currency rates and rising dining costs abroad. For families, these deals are often found in our guide to budget family beach holidays in Europe.

In 2026, the definition of "All-Inclusive" is expanding. While the core promise of unlimited meals and local drinks remains, many budget-friendly resorts now include premium extras like water sports, guided local tours, and kids' clubs within the headline price. By choosing the right destination—such as the emerging resorts in Albania or the established value hubs of Turkey—you can enjoy a high-end resort experience for a fraction of the cost of a traditional DIY beach holiday. This guide explores how to identify genuine value and which destinations are leading the pack for the year ahead.

Planning an all-inclusive trip requires a shift in mindset. Instead of looking for the lowest flight price, you should be looking for the best overall package "yield"—the total value of what is included vs the upfront cost. Whether you're flying from London, Manchester, or Edinburgh, there are specific "value windows" throughout the year where all-inclusive prices drop significantly. We've analyzed thousands of packages to bring you the data-driven insights you need to book your next stress-free getaway with confidence.

What’s included in all-inclusive holidays?

While every resort varies, a standard "Budget All-Inclusive" package from the UK in 2026 typically includes:

  • Flights: Return economy flights with a 15-22kg baggage allowance (if booked as a package).
  • Accommodation: Usually a 3 or 4-star resort with access to pools and beach areas.
  • Meals: Breakfast, lunch, and dinner, typically served buffet-style in a main restaurant.
  • Drinks: Unlimited local spirits, beer, wine, and soft drinks (usually from 10 am to 11 pm).
  • Entertainment: Daily poolside activities and evening shows or live music.
  • Transfers: Shared coach transfers from the airport to your hotel (check your booking).

Best Time to Book All-Inclusive

Timing your booking is critical for securing the lowest rates. For all-inclusive packages, the "early bird" strategy often wins:

  • The "January Window": This is when major operators like Jet2 and TUI launch their biggest sales. Booking here for the summer can save you up to 30%.
  • The 8-Week Sweet Spot: If you missed the early sales, prices often dip again around 8-10 weeks before departure as operators look to fill remaining seats.
  • Last-Minute (Under 14 Days): High risk, but potentially very high reward. Prices can be slashed by 50% if unsold rooms remain.

How To Find The Cheapest All Inclusive Deals

  • Compare multiple travel providers
  • Check airline + hotel package deals
  • Look at midweek departure dates
  • Consider flying from alternative UK airports

Are All-Inclusive Holidays Worth It?

For families and travellers who plan to spend most of their time at the resort, all inclusive holidays often provide predictable costs and good overall value compared to paying for meals and drinks separately. If you enjoy having a "fixed price" for your holiday before you even leave home, all-inclusive is unbeatable. However, if you prefer to explore local restaurants and move around frequently, a DIY city break to somewhere like Rome or Paris might be better value for you.

Final Budget Tip

Flexibility is the biggest money saver. Travellers who can shift travel dates by a few days often unlock significantly cheaper all inclusive deals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to book all-inclusive holidays from the UK?

The best time to book is during the January sales for summer travel, or 6–8 weeks before departure for shoulder season discounts.

How much does an all-inclusive holiday from the UK cost?

A budget all-inclusive holiday from the UK typically costs between £350 and £600 per person for 7 nights.

Which UK airports have the best all-inclusive holiday deals?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes for all-inclusive holidays.

How far in advance should I book a cheap all-inclusive holiday?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance is usually ideal, but the January sales period often offers the deepest discounts.

Is all-inclusive better value than booking flights and hotels separately?

For those who plan to spend most of their time at the resort, all-inclusive almost always provides better value than paying as you go.
