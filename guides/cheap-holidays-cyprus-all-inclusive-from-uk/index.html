Cheap All Inclusive Holidays To Cyprus From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Cyprus has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. Whether you are planning to explore the legendary Aphrodite's Rock, visit the historic Tombs of the Kings, or discover the vibrant Nicosia Old City, this location offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Cyprus provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. If you enjoy Mediterranean island history, you might also like budget breaks in Rhodes. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on sun-drenched beaches and ancient Mediterranean history, you can uncover the true essence of Cyprus while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control. Cheapest Time To Visit Cyprus

The cheapest months to visit Cyprus from the UK are:

  • November to March
  • April and May

Cyprus has one of the longest summer seasons in Europe, making it perfect for a cheap early or late-season sun break.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester
  • Birmingham
  • Bristol

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £50–£160 return
  • All-inclusive packages: £450–£800
  • Daily budget (if not all-in): £40–£70

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Book your all-inclusive package 6–9 months in advance for 'early bird' discounts
  • Look for resorts slightly outside the main town centers for better prices
  • Choose 'All-Inclusive Light' if you don't need unlimited alcoholic drinks
  • Use the local buses to visit free archaeological sites and beaches

Best Areas to Stay in Cyprus for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Cyprus. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the tourist hub of Paphos, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Cyprus, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the lively Ayia Napa or the coastal Limassol, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Cyprus that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Cyprus

Budget travellers visiting Cyprus can typically expect to spend between £50 and £80 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale. Local meals in Cyprus are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore Cyprus extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Cyprus from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Cyprus is typically during November to March and April and May, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Cyprus cost from the UK?

Flights: £50–£160 return. All-inclusive packages: £450–£800. Daily budget (if not all-in): £40–£70

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Cyprus?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Cyprus?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Cyprus holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Cyprus due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
