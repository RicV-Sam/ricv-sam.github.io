Cheap Holidays To Budapest From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Budapest from the UK is a masterclass in value travel, offering a majestic European capital experience at a fraction of the cost of London or Paris. From the historic Széchenyi Thermal Bath to the iconic Parliament building on the Danube, the "Pearl of the Danube" is as affordable as it is beautiful. For UK holidaymakers, the abundance of budget flights from regional airports makes Budapest a prime choice for a 2026 city break.

The secret to an affordable Budapest trip is embracing its unique local culture. You can spend your days wandering the fairytale turrets of Fisherman's Bastion or exploring the legendary ruin bars of the Jewish Quarter, knowing your pound goes significantly further here. If you enjoy cities with a grand, historic feel, you might also like budget trips to Vienna (if available) or cheap holidays to Prague. By eating at local markets and using the historic tram network, you can craft a world-class itinerary that respects your budget while delivering an unforgettable Hungarian experience.

Cheapest Time To Visit Budapest

The cheapest months to visit Budapest are January to March and the month of November.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Luton

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £35–£120 return from major UK hubs
  • Hotels: £50–£125 per night for a central apartment or boutique hotel
  • City break packages: £180–£360 for a 3-night break

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Ride Tram Line 2: Instead of an expensive river cruise, take Tram Line 2. It's one of the most scenic tram routes in the world, running along the Danube past the Parliament and Chain Bridge for the price of a standard ticket.
  • Eat Like a Local at the Central Market: Head to the Nagyvásárcsarnok (Central Market Hall) and visit the upper floor for authentic, cheap Hungarian street food like Lángos (deep-fried dough with toppings).
  • Explore Ruin Bars by Day: Legendary ruin bars like Szimpla Kert are free to enter and wander through during the day. You can enjoy the incredible decor without the nighttime crowds or drink prices.
  • Free Views from Gellért Hill: Skip the expensive viewpoints and hike up Gellért Hill for the best panoramic views of the city and the Danube at sunset for free.

Best Areas to Stay in Budapest for Budget Travellers

For the best value and atmosphere, consider staying in these central but affordable districts:

  • District VII (Jewish Quarter): The heart of Budapest's social scene, offering a huge range of budget-friendly hostels, trendy apartments, and the city's famous ruin bars.
  • District VIII (Palace District): A rapidly up-and-coming area with grand historic buildings, great-value cafes, and a more local, less touristy feel.
  • District VI (Terézváros): Look at the streets between Andrássy Avenue and the Jewish Quarter for well-priced guesthouses and excellent transport links.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Budapest

Expect to spend **£45 to £85 per day**. This is one of the best value cities in Europe, where a large beer can be under £2 and a decent meal under £10. Your biggest expense will likely be the thermal baths (around £15-20), but they are worth every penny. For another top-value city in Eastern Europe, see our guide to budget travel in Krakow.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Budapest from the UK?

January to March is the most affordable period for a visit to the Hungarian capital.

How much does a holiday to Budapest cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£130 return. Hotels: £60–£140 per night. City break packages: £190–£380

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Budapest?

Budapest Ferenc Liszt International (BUD) is well-served by Wizz Air and Ryanair from across the UK.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Budapest?

Booking 6–8 weeks in advance is ideal for securing the best rates on central apartments.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

DIY booking is incredibly popular for Budapest due to the abundance of cheap flights and affordable private rentals.
