Cheap Holidays To Budapest From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

For many UK holidaymakers, Budapest represents the perfect blend of exotic allure and wallet-friendly practicality. From the moment you arrive to see the majestic Parliament Building or wander around the historic Széchenyi Thermal Bath, you'll realize why this has become such a popular choice for those looking to maximize their travel budget. With frequent flights and diverse package options departing from regional UK airports, the journey to Budapest is both convenient and surprisingly economical.

What truly sets Budapest apart is the wealth of low-cost activities and dining options available. You can spend your days visiting the iconic Fisherman's Bastion or simply soaking in the local atmosphere, knowing that your money goes much further here than in typical European capitals. By prioritizing historic thermal baths and legendary ruin bars and opting for authentic local experiences, you can craft a memorable itinerary that respects your financial boundaries without sacrificing any of the magic.

Cheapest Time To Visit Budapest

The cheapest months to visit Budapest are January to March and the month of November.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Luton
  • Manchester
  • Edinburgh
  • Birmingham

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £40–£130 return
  • Hotels: £60–£140 per night
  • City break packages: £190–£380

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • The #2 Tram is one of the most beautiful (and cheapest) scenic routes in the world.
  • Visit the Great Market Hall (Nagyvásárcsarnok) for cheap street food like Lángos.
  • Ruin bars like Szimpla Kert are free to enter and explore during the day.
  • Use the 'BKK' app to manage your transport tickets easily on your phone.

Best Areas to Stay in Budapest for Budget Travellers

The **Jewish Quarter (District VII)** is the heart of the budget scene, home to most hostels and ruin bars. For something slightly quieter but still central and affordable, look at **District VIII (Palace District)** or the southern parts of **District VI**. These areas offer grand architecture and local prices.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Budapest

Expect to spend **£45 to £85 per day**. This is one of the best value cities in Europe, where a large beer can be under £2 and a decent meal under £10. Your biggest expense will likely be the thermal baths (around £15-20), but they are worth every penny.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Budapest from the UK?

January to March is the most affordable period for a visit to the Hungarian capital.

How much does a holiday to Budapest cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£130 return. Hotels: £60–£140 per night. City break packages: £190–£380

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Budapest?

Budapest Ferenc Liszt International (BUD) is well-served by Wizz Air and Ryanair from across the UK.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Budapest?

Booking 6–8 weeks in advance is ideal for securing the best rates on central apartments.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

DIY booking is incredibly popular for Budapest due to the abundance of cheap flights and affordable private rentals.
