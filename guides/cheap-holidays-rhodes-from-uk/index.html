Cheap Holidays To Rhodes From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Rhodes has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. Whether you are planning to explore the medieval Old Town of Rhodes, visit the stunning village of Lindos, or discover the ancient Acropolis of Lindos, this location offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Rhodes provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on medieval fortifications and sun-drenched beaches, you can uncover the true essence of Rhodes while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control.

Cheapest Time To Visit Rhodes

The cheapest months to visit Rhodes from the UK are:

  • April to June
  • September to November

Rhodes stays warm late into the autumn, making it a great choice for a cheap late-season sun break.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Gatwick
  • London Stansted
  • Manchester
  • Birmingham
  • Liverpool

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £60–£170 return
  • Hotels: £35–£75 per night
  • Entry to Rhodes Castle: £6

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Use the 'KTEL' buses to travel between Rhodes Town, Faliraki, and Lindos
  • Stay in self-catering studios in Faliraki for the best value rooms
  • Explore the Rhodes Old Town on foot—it's like an open-air museum for free
  • Look for 'Greek Nights' in smaller villages for affordable food and entertainment

Best Areas to Stay in Rhodes for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Rhodes. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the historic Rhodes Town, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Rhodes, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the charming Lindos or the social hub of Faliraki, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Rhodes that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Rhodes

Budget travellers visiting Rhodes can typically expect to spend between £50 and £80 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale.

Local meals in Rhodes are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore Rhodes extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Rhodes from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Rhodes is typically during April to June and September to November, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Rhodes cost from the UK?

Flights: £60–£170 return. Hotels: £35–£75 per night. Entry to Rhodes Castle: £6

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Rhodes?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Rhodes?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Rhodes holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Rhodes due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
