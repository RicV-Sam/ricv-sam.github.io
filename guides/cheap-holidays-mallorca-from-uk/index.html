Cheap Holidays To Mallorca From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Mallorca from the UK is the ultimate Mediterranean all-rounder, offering everything from chic city breaks in Palma to pristine turquoise bays (calas) and dramatic mountain scenery. While it has a reputation for luxury, Mallorca remains one of the best-value destinations in Europe if you know where to look. For UK travellers, the sheer volume of budget flights from across the country makes it a prime candidate for a 2026 value sun break.

The secret to an affordable Mallorca trip is embracing the island's diverse regions. Spend your days exploring the majestic Palma Cathedral or hiking the UNESCO-listed Serra de Tramuntana mountains for free. If you enjoy the Spanish islands, you might also like budget holidays to Tenerife. By staying in authentic towns like Alcudia or regional hubs like Palma and utilizing the island's excellent bus network, you can experience the true essence of Balearic life while keeping your holiday costs firmly under control.

Best Areas to Stay in Mallorca for Budget Travellers

To find the best value and avoid the highest prices of the luxury resorts, consider these areas:

  • Alcudia (Old Town): Staying in or near the historic walled town offers a more authentic atmosphere and better-value accommodation than the neighboring port area.
  • Palma (Off-Centre): Look for guesthouses in areas like Santa Catalina or near Plaza España for easy transport links and local prices for dining and drinks.
  • Cala Millor: A popular family resort on the east coast that often features very competitive package prices and a wide range of budget-friendly apartments.

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Use the TIB Bus Network: Mallorca's red-and-yellow buses (TIB) are modern, efficient, and very cheap. Use your contactless card to 'tap on and tap off' for the lowest fares across the island.
  • Eat a 'Menú del Día': Look for local restaurants away from the seafront offering a fixed-price three-course lunch. It's the most affordable way to enjoy authentic Mallorcan cuisine.
  • Explore Palma on Foot: The historic center of Palma is compact and beautiful. Spend a day wandering the narrow streets and visiting the Arab Baths for a low-cost cultural experience.
  • Visit the Free Markets: Towns like Sineu and Pollença host vibrant weekly markets. They are free to explore and are great places to pick up fresh local produce for a budget-friendly picnic.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Mallorca

Budget travellers visiting Mallorca can typically expect to spend between £50 and £80 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale. Local meals in Mallorca are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore Mallorca extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Mallorca from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Mallorca is typically during April–June and September–October, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Mallorca cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£140 return. Hotels: £50–£110 per night. Package holidays: £300–£550

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Mallorca?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Mallorca?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Mallorca holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Mallorca due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
