Cheap Holidays To Rome From The UK

1 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Rome, the Eternal City, is a treasure trove of history that can be surprisingly affordable if you know where to look. With a flight time of around 2.5 hours, it's a popular choice for UK travellers seeking art, culture, and world-class food.

While the main tourist sites have entry fees, much of Rome's beauty—from the Trevi Fountain to the Pantheon—can be enjoyed for free. Exploring neighbourhoods like Trastevere will reveal more authentic and cheaper dining options.

Cheapest Time To Visit Rome

November to February (excluding the Christmas period) is the cheapest time to visit Rome from the UK.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester
  • Birmingham
  • Bristol

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £50–£150 return
  • Hotels: £70–£160 per night
  • City break packages: £250–£450

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Use the 'nasoni' free water fountains
  • Buy a Roma Pass if visiting multiple museums
  • Eat pizza al taglio for a cheap and quick lunch
  • Visit the Vatican Museums on the last Sunday of the month (free entry)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Rome from the UK?

November through February offers the lowest prices, with the exception of the holiday weeks.

How much does a holiday to Rome cost from the UK?

Flights: £50–£150 return. Hotels: £70–£160 per night. City break packages: £250–£450

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Rome?

Both Ciampino (CIA) and Fiumicino (FCO) receive budget flights from across the UK.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Rome?

Aim to book 2–3 months in advance, especially if you want to stay near the historic centre.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

DIY booking often works well for Rome, allowing you to pair low-cost flights with boutique guesthouses.
