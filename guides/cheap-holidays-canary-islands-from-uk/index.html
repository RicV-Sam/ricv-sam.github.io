Cheap Holidays To Canary Islands From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

For many UK holidaymakers, the Canary Islands represents the perfect blend of exotic allure and wallet-friendly practicality. From the moment you arrive to see the volcanic peaks of Mount Teide or wander around the sand dunes of Maspalomas, you'll realize why this has become such a popular choice for those looking to maximize their travel budget. With frequent flights and diverse package options departing from regional UK airports, the journey to the Canary Islands is both convenient and surprisingly economical.

What truly sets the Canary Islands apart is the wealth of low-cost activities and dining options available. You can spend your days visiting the lunar landscapes of Lanzarote or simply soaking in the local atmosphere, knowing that your money goes much further here than in typical European capitals. By prioritizing year-round sunshine and diverse volcanic terrains and opting for authentic local experiences, you can craft a memorable itinerary that respects your financial boundaries without sacrificing any of the magic.

Cheapest Time To Visit Canary Islands

The cheapest months to visit the Canary Islands from the UK are:

  • January and February (excluding half-term)
  • May and June
  • November

Avoiding school holidays is the key to finding the lowest prices for these popular islands.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Luton
  • Manchester
  • Birmingham
  • East Midlands
  • Glasgow

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £50–£150 return
  • Hotels: £40–£80 per night
  • All-inclusive packages: £400–£750

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Duty-free status means alcohol, tobacco, and perfume are very cheap
  • Rent a car to explore the interior—it's often cheaper than organized tours
  • Eat at 'Guachinches' in Tenerife for authentic and cheap local food
  • Use the reliable 'TITSA' bus network in Tenerife or 'Global' in Gran Canaria

Best Areas to Stay in the Canary Islands for Budget Travellers

When it comes to finding affordable lodging in the Canary Islands, location is everything. Savvy travellers often bypass the expensive city centres in favour of districts like Los Cristianos in Tenerife, where you can find much better value for your money. These neighbourhoods usually boast excellent transport links, making it a breeze to commute to the main sights while enjoying a quieter, more local environment at night.

Areas such as Las Palmas in Gran Canaria and Puerto del Carmen in Lanzarote are particularly well-suited for those watching their pennies. Here, the concentration of budget-friendly hostels and simple apartments is higher, and the local dining scene reflects the more modest prices of the residents. Staying in these parts of the Canary Islands allows you to stretch your budget further, giving you more freedom to spend on experiences and attractions rather than just a place to sleep.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in the Canary Islands

While your total spend in the Canary Islands will vary based on your travel style, most budget-conscious visitors find that £50 and £80 a day is a realistic target. This budget allows for a clean and safe hostel bed, several local meals, and enough left over for public transport and one or two modest paid attractions. You can further optimize your spending by taking advantage of lunch specials and free museum days which are common in many parts of the Canary Islands.

The cost of food and transport in the Canary Islands is generally quite reasonable, particularly if you steer clear of the most tourist-heavy plazas. Local buses and trains offer a cost-effective way to get around, and street food vendors provide a delicious and cheap alternative to sit-down dining. By setting a daily limit and sticking to it, you can enjoy all the highlights of the Canary Islands without any financial stress during or after your trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Canary Islands from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Canary Islands is typically during January and February (excluding half-term), May and June and November, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Canary Islands cost from the UK?

Flights: £50–£150 return. Hotels: £40–£80 per night. All-inclusive packages: £400–£750

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Canary Islands?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Canary Islands?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Canary Islands holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Canary Islands due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
