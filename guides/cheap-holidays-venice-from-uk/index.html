Cheap Holidays To Venice From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Venice has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. Whether you are planning to explore the majestic St. Mark's Basilica, visit the historic Rialto Bridge, or discover the iconic Doge's Palace, this location offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Venice provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on a floating city of canals and Renaissance art, you can uncover the true essence of Venice while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control.

Cheapest Time To Visit Venice

The cheapest months are November and January, avoiding the busy Carnival period in February.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Gatwick
  • London Stansted
  • Manchester
  • Bristol
  • Edinburgh

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £45–£140 return
  • Hotels: £80–£180 per night
  • City break packages: £280–£450

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Eat 'Cicchetti'—Venice's answer to tapas—at local 'Baccari' (wine bars). It's the most authentic and affordable way to dine.
  • Take the #1 Vaporetto down the Grand Canal for a DIY tour that costs just a few Euros instead of a €80 gondola ride.
  • Use the 'Traghetto' to cross the Grand Canal; these are gondolas used as ferries at specific points and cost only €2 for tourists.
  • Fill your water bottle at the public fountains (fontanelle) found in almost every campo to save on bottled water prices.

Best Areas to Stay in Venice for Budget Travellers

Avoid staying in San Marco or near the Rialto Bridge. Instead, look for accommodation in **Cannaregio** (the old Jewish Ghetto area) or **Castello**. These districts are much more authentic, quieter, and offer significantly better value for money while still being within walking distance of the main sights.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Venice

Expect to spend **£80 to £135 per day**. This covers a modest guesthouse or religious-run stay, a 24-hour Vaporetto pass, and plenty of delicious 'cicchetti' (Venetian tapas) and local prosecco. Many of Venice's most beautiful churches house incredible art and are free or very cheap to enter.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Venice from the UK?

November and January are typically the most budget-friendly months for a Venice trip.

How much does a holiday to Venice cost from the UK?

Flights: £45–£140 return. Hotels: £80–£180 per night. City break packages: £280–£450

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Venice?

Marco Polo (VCE) is the main airport, while Treviso (TSF) is a common base for low-cost airlines.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Venice?

Booking 3 months in advance is ideal for finding affordable hotels on the island itself.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

DIY booking is usually cheaper, particularly if you choose to stay slightly away from the main tourist hubs.
