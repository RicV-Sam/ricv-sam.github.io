Cheap Holidays To Dubrovnik From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

For many UK holidaymakers, Dubrovnik represents the perfect blend of exotic allure and wallet-friendly practicality. From the moment you arrive to see the historic Old Town walls or wander around the stunning Stradun, you'll realize why this has become such a popular choice for those looking to maximize their travel budget. With frequent flights and diverse package options departing from regional UK airports, the journey to Dubrovnik is both convenient and surprisingly economical.

What truly sets Dubrovnik apart is the wealth of low-cost activities and dining options available. You can spend your days visiting the scenic Mount Srđ cable car or simply soaking in the local atmosphere, knowing that your money goes much further here than in typical European capitals. By prioritizing remarkably preserved medieval architecture and crystal-clear Adriatic waters and opting for authentic local experiences, you can craft a memorable itinerary that respects your financial boundaries without sacrificing any of the magic.

Cheapest Time To Visit Dubrovnik

April, May, and October are the most affordable months to visit Dubrovnik.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester
  • Birmingham
  • Edinburgh

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £80–£200 return
  • Hotels: £70–£150 per night
  • Holiday packages: £450–£650

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Buy the Dubrovnik Pass for entry to the walls and museums
  • Stay in Lapad or Babin Kuk for better value accommodation
  • Take the local bus from the airport instead of a taxi
  • Enjoy a picnic on the rocks at Danče beach

Best Areas to Stay in Dubrovnik for Budget Travellers

When it comes to finding affordable lodging in Dubrovnik, location is everything. Savvy travellers often bypass the expensive city centres in favour of districts like the historic Lapad Peninsula, where you can find much better value for your money. These neighbourhoods usually boast excellent transport links, making it a breeze to commute to the main sights while enjoying a quieter, more local environment at night.

Areas such as the bustling Gruž port area and the charming Babin Kuk are particularly well-suited for those watching their pennies. Here, the concentration of budget-friendly hostels and simple apartments is higher, and the local dining scene reflects the more modest prices of the residents. Staying in these parts of Dubrovnik allows you to stretch your budget further, giving you more freedom to spend on experiences and attractions rather than just a place to sleep.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Dubrovnik

While your total spend in Dubrovnik will vary based on your travel style, most budget-conscious visitors find that £80 and £130 a day is a realistic target. This budget allows for a clean and safe hostel bed, several local meals, and enough left over for public transport and one or two modest paid attractions. You can further optimize your spending by taking advantage of lunch specials and free museum days which are common in many parts of Dubrovnik.

The cost of food and transport in Dubrovnik is generally quite reasonable, particularly if you steer clear of the most tourist-heavy plazas. Local buses and trains offer a cost-effective way to get around, and street food vendors provide a delicious and cheap alternative to sit-down dining. By setting a daily limit and sticking to it, you can enjoy all the highlights of Dubrovnik without any financial stress during or after your trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Dubrovnik from the UK?

April and October are the cheapest months, offering a balance of mild weather and lower costs.

How much does a holiday to Dubrovnik cost from the UK?

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Dubrovnik?

Dubrovnik Airport (DBV) is served by several low-cost carriers from across the UK.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Dubrovnik?

Booking at least 4 months in advance is vital for finding budget-friendly stays in this popular city.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Packages can often be cheaper for Dubrovnik, as they may include hotels that are difficult to book affordably on their own.
