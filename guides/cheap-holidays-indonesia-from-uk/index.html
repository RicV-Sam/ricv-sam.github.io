Cheap Holidays To Indonesia From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

For many UK holidaymakers, Indonesia represents the perfect blend of exotic allure and wallet-friendly practicality. From the moment you arrive to see the ancient Borobudur temple or wander around the iconic Mount Bromo, you'll realize why this has become such a popular choice for those looking to maximize their travel budget. With frequent flights and diverse package options departing from regional UK airports, the journey to Indonesia is both convenient and surprisingly economical.

What truly sets Indonesia apart is the wealth of low-cost activities and dining options available. You can spend your days visiting the pristine Gili Islands or simply soaking in the local atmosphere, knowing that your money goes much further here than in typical European capitals. If you are looking for specific island tips, see our guide to budget travel in Bali. By prioritizing incredible biodiversity and thousands of tropical islands and opting for authentic local experiences, you can craft a memorable itinerary that respects your financial boundaries without sacrificing any of the magic. Those exploring Southeast Asia should also see our budget guide to Thailand.

Cheapest Time To Visit Indonesia

The cheapest months to visit Indonesia from the UK are:

  • October to March (excluding Christmas)

The rainy season offers the best deals on flights and hotels, though you should expect afternoon showers.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Heathrow
  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £650–£950 return
  • Hotels/Guesthouses: £10–£35 per night
  • Nasi Goreng (meal): £2–£4

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Eat at 'Warungs' for authentic and very cheap local food
  • Use local ferries to travel between islands instead of flights
  • Stay in 'Losmen' (traditional guesthouses) for the best value
  • Use the 'Gojek' or 'Grab' apps for cheap transport and food delivery

Best Areas to Stay in Indonesia for Budget Travellers

When it comes to finding affordable lodging in Indonesia, location is everything. Savvy travellers often bypass the expensive city centres in favour of districts like the cultural Ubud in Bali, where you can find much better value for your money. These neighbourhoods usually boast excellent transport links, making it a breeze to commute to the main sights while enjoying a quieter, more local environment at night.

Areas such as vibrant South Bali and the tropical Gili Islands are particularly well-suited for those watching their pennies. Here, the concentration of budget-friendly hostels and simple apartments is higher, and the local dining scene reflects the more modest prices of the residents. Staying in these parts of Indonesia allows you to stretch your budget further, giving you more freedom to spend on experiences and attractions rather than just a place to sleep.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Indonesia

While your total spend in Indonesia will vary based on your travel style, most budget-conscious visitors find that £30 and £50 a day is a realistic target. This budget allows for a clean and safe hostel bed, several local meals, and enough left over for public transport and one or two modest paid attractions. You can further optimize your spending by taking advantage of lunch specials and free museum days which are common in many parts of Indonesia. For more long-haul value, check out our guide to affordable holidays in Vietnam.

The cost of food and transport in Indonesia is generally quite reasonable, particularly if you steer clear of the most tourist-heavy plazas. Local buses and trains offer a cost-effective way to get around, and street food vendors provide a delicious and cheap alternative to sit-down dining. By setting a daily limit and sticking to it, you can enjoy all the highlights of Indonesia without any financial stress during or after your trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Indonesia from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Indonesia is typically during October to March (excluding Christmas), when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Indonesia cost from the UK?

Flights: £650–£950 return. Hotels/Guesthouses: £10–£35 per night. Nasi Goreng (meal): £2–£4

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Indonesia?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Indonesia?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Indonesia holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Indonesia due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
