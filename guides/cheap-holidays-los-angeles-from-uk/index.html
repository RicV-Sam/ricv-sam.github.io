Cheap Holidays To Los Angeles From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Los Angeles has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. Whether you are planning to explore the iconic Hollywood Sign, visit the historic Santa Monica Pier, or discover the world-class Getty Center, LA offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and value. If you're touring the US, you might also like budget travel in Las Vegas. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Los Angeles provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on entertainment industry heart and diverse cultural districts, you can uncover the true essence of Los Angeles while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control.

Cheapest Time To Visit Los Angeles

The cheapest months to visit Los Angeles from the UK are:

  • January to March
  • October and November

Winter in LA is mild and pleasant, with significantly lower hotel rates than the summer peak.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Heathrow
  • London Gatwick

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £350–£600 return
  • Hotels: £80–£150 per night
  • Taco Truck Meal: £8–£12

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Visit the Griffith Observatory for incredible free views of the Hollywood sign
  • Eat at the Grand Central Market for affordable and diverse food options
  • Use the Metro Rail system where possible to save on parking and Uber costs
  • Enjoy the free beaches at Santa Monica, Venice, and Malibu

Best Areas to Stay in Los Angeles for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Los Angeles. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the historic Hollywood, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Los Angeles, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring coastal Santa Monica or the revamped Downtown LA, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Los Angeles that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Los Angeles

Budget travellers visiting Los Angeles can typically expect to spend between £80 and £130 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale. Local meals in Los Angeles are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore Los Angeles extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact.
