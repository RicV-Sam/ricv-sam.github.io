Cheap Holidays To Krakow From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Krakow has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. Whether you are planning to explore the historic Wawel Royal Castle, visit the sprawling Main Market Square, or discover the somber Kazimierz district, this location offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Krakow provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on medieval architecture and a vibrant student-led culture, you can uncover the true essence of Krakow while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control.

Cheapest Time To Visit Krakow

January, February, and November are the cheapest months to visit Krakow from the UK.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Luton
  • Manchester

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £40–£120 return
  • Hotels: £55–£130 per night
  • City break packages: £180–£350

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Eat at 'Milk Bars' (Bar Mleczny) for traditional, cheap food
  • Take a free walking tour to learn about the city's history
  • Walk along the Vistula river for great views of Wawel Castle
  • Use the frequent and cheap train from the airport to the centre

Best Areas to Stay in Krakow for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Krakow. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the historic Old Town, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Krakow, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the trendy Kazimierz or the upcoming Podgórze, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Krakow that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Krakow

Budget travellers visiting Krakow can typically expect to spend between £30 and £50 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale.

Local meals in Krakow are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore Krakow extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact.
