Cheap Holidays To Malta From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

For many UK holidaymakers, Malta represents the perfect blend of exotic allure and wallet-friendly practicality. If you enjoy Mediterranean history, you might also like budget travel in Italy. From the moment you arrive to see the historic city of Valletta or wander around the ancient Ħaġar Qim temples, you'll realize why this has become such a popular choice for those looking to maximize their travel budget. With frequent flights and diverse package options departing from regional UK airports, the journey to Malta is both convenient and surprisingly economical.

What truly sets Malta apart is the wealth of low-cost activities and dining options available. You can spend your days visiting the stunning Blue Lagoon or simply soaking in the local atmosphere, knowing that your money goes much further here than in typical European capitals. By prioritizing remarkable prehistoric temples and Baroque architecture and opting for authentic local experiences, you can craft a memorable itinerary that respects your financial boundaries without sacrificing any of the magic.

Cheapest Time To Visit Malta

The cheapest months to visit Malta from the UK are:

  • November to March
  • April and May

Spring is particularly beautiful as the islands are green and the weather is mild for exploring.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Stansted
  • London Luton
  • Manchester
  • East Midlands
  • Bournemouth

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £40–£120 return
  • Hotels: £30–£70 per night
  • PastiZZi (local snack): £0.50

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Use the extensive and cheap bus network (get a Tallinja card)
  • Eat 'Pastizzi' for a delicious and very cheap local lunch
  • Many museums and churches have free entry or low costs
  • Buy a multi-day ferry pass for Valletta and the Three Cities

Best Areas to Stay in Malta for Budget Travellers

When it comes to finding affordable lodging in Malta, location is everything. Savvy travellers often bypass the expensive city centres in favour of districts like the coastal Sliema, where you can find much better value for your money. These neighbourhoods usually boast excellent transport links, making it a breeze to commute to the main sights while enjoying a quieter, more local environment at night.

Areas such as the lively St. Julian's and the historic Three Cities are particularly well-suited for those watching their pennies. Here, the concentration of budget-friendly hostels and simple apartments is higher, and the local dining scene reflects the more modest prices of the residents. Staying in these parts of Malta allows you to stretch your budget further, giving you more freedom to spend on experiences and attractions rather than just a place to sleep.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Malta

While your total spend in Malta will vary based on your travel style, most budget-conscious visitors find that £50 and £80 a day is a realistic target. This budget allows for a clean and safe hostel bed, several local meals, and enough left over for public transport and one or two modest paid attractions. You can further optimize your spending by taking advantage of lunch specials and free museum days which are common in many parts of Malta. For more island value, see our guide to budget travel in Cyprus.

The cost of food and transport in Malta is generally quite reasonable, particularly if you steer clear of the most tourist-heavy plazas. Local buses and trains offer a cost-effective way to get around, and street food vendors provide a delicious and cheap alternative to sit-down dining. By setting a daily limit and sticking to it, you can enjoy all the highlights of Malta without any financial stress during or after your trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Malta from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Malta is typically during November to March and April and May, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Malta cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£120 return. Hotels: £30–£70 per night. PastiZZi (local snack): £0.50

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Malta?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Malta?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Malta holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Malta due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
