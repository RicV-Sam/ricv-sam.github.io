Cheap Holidays To Paris From The UK | Budget Travel Guide

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Exploring Paris on a budget is not only possible but incredibly rewarding for savvy travellers from the UK. Whether your heart is set on witnessing the iconic Eiffel Tower, exploring the depths of the world-famous Louvre Museum, or experiencing the historic Notre-Dame Cathedral, you'll find that this destination caters exceptionally well to the cost-conscious. The accessibility provided by a wide range of airline partners means that finding a deal to Paris is often just a matter of timing and a bit of research.

Once you've touched down, Paris reveals its true value through its affordable infrastructure and local charm. The availability of unmatched artistic heritage and world-class patisseries ensures that every day is filled with wonder, regardless of your daily spend. By engaging with the local culture and choosing to stay in traditional neighbourhoods, you'll gain a deeper appreciation for Paris while enjoying a holiday that is as affordable as it is unforgettable.

Cheapest Time To Visit Paris

The cheapest months to visit Paris from the UK are:

  • January to March

The post-Christmas period and late winter months offer the lowest prices for accommodation and travel, as well as fewer crowds at major attractions.

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Use the RER train from Charles de Gaulle airport instead of a taxi
  • Look for "Bouillons"—traditional French restaurants that offer classic dishes at very low prices
  • Many major attractions are free for those under 26 who are EU residents (check current post-Brexit status for UK residents)
  • Enjoy free views of the city from the rooftop of Galeries Lafayette

Best Areas to Stay in Paris for Budget Travellers

Finding the perfect base in Paris doesn't have to break the bank if you know where to look. For the best deals, focus your search on up-and-coming neighbourhoods like the bohemian Montmartre, which offer a great selection of budget accommodation. These spots are typically just a short hop from the main tourist zones, providing the perfect balance between convenience and cost-effectiveness.

Furthermore, districts like the historic Latin Quarter and the vibrant Bastille are renowned for their student populations and local markets, which translates to affordable beds and even cheaper food. By choosing to stay in these lively, authentic areas, you'll benefit from a more immersive experience in Paris while keeping your accommodation costs remarkably low. It's an excellent strategy for anyone looking to see the best of Paris on a strict budget.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Paris

To enjoy Paris without overspending, a daily budget of approximately £80 and £130 is usually sufficient for the average backpacker or budget tourist. This figure accounts for basic accommodation, local transport, and satisfying meals from local eateries. It's a great destination for those who want a rich cultural experience without the high price tags found in other global hotspots, provided you make smart choices about where you eat and how you move around.

Transport costs in Paris are often one of the lowest parts of a traveller's budget, thanks to an extensive network of affordable local options. Similarly, food costs can be kept low by visiting local markets and bakeries for breakfast and lunch. By allocating your funds wisely and prioritizing the sights that matter most to you, you'll find that Paris offers incredible value for every pound spent.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Paris from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Paris is typically during January to March, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Paris cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£120 return. Hotels: £70–£180 per night. Package holidays: £150–£350

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Paris?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Paris?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Paris holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Paris due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
