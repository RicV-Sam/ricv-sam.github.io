Cheap Holidays To Paris From The UK | Budget Travel Guide

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Paris doesn't have to be the city of expensive champagne and designer labels; it's also a city of world-class boulangeries and free hilltop views. For UK visitors, the French capital offers an accessible slice of continental elegance that can be enjoyed on a surprisingly modest budget if you know where the locals linger.

Walking through the historic Marais or climbing the steps to the Sacré-Cœur in Montmartre costs nothing but provides the classic Parisian experience. By swapping high-end bistros for local 'formule' lunch menus and utilizing the extensive Metro network, you can experience the magic of the Eiffel Tower and the Louvre without the financial hangover.

Cheapest Time To Visit Paris

The cheapest months to visit Paris from the UK are:

  • January to March

The post-Christmas period and late winter months offer the lowest prices for accommodation and travel, as well as fewer crowds at major attractions.

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £40–£120 return
  • Hotels: £70–£180 per night
  • Package holidays: £150–£350

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Buy a 'Carnet' of 10 Metro tickets or use a Navigo Easy card for better rates.
  • Get your wine and cheese from a local Monoprix and picnic at the Champ de Mars.
  • Most museums are free for under-26s (check your eligibility post-Brexit).
  • Climb the Arc de Triomphe instead of the Eiffel Tower for better views including the Tower itself.

Best Areas to Stay in Paris for Budget Travellers

Skip the 1st and 8th arrondissements for sleeping. Instead, head to **Montmartre (18th)** for its artistic history and cheaper hostels, or the **Latin Quarter (5th)** for its student-friendly prices and lively atmosphere. The **Canal Saint-Martin (10th)** area is also excellent for budget travellers seeking a trendy, less touristy base.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Paris

A daily budget of **£90 to £145** is realistic. This allows for a Metro pass, a few museum entries (many are free on the first Sunday of the month), and dining at traditional 'Bouillons' like Chartier, where three-course meals are incredibly affordable.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Paris from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Paris is typically during January to March, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Paris cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£120 return. Hotels: £70–£180 per night. Package holidays: £150–£350

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Paris?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Paris?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Paris holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Paris due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
