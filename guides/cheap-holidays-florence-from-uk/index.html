Cheap Holidays To Florence From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Exploring Florence on a budget is not only possible but incredibly rewarding for savvy travellers from the UK. Whether your heart is set on witnessing the iconic Duomo, visiting the world-famous Uffizi Gallery, or discovering the historic Ponte Vecchio, Florence offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. If you're visiting Italy, also consider cheap holidays to Rome from the UK, which can easily be combined with a trip to Tuscany.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Florence provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food like 'lampredotto' and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. For those looking for more Italian inspiration, our budget guide to Venice offers tips for another iconic art city nearby.

Cheapest Time To Visit Florence

January, February, and November are the most affordable months for a Florence city break.

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £55–£160 return
  • Hotels: £75–£170 per night
  • City break packages: £280–£450

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Visit the free-to-enter churches for stunning art
  • Eat panini from famous stalls like All'Antico Vinaio
  • Watch the sunset from Piazzale Michelangelo for the best free view
  • Consider flying to Pisa and taking the cheap train to Florence

Best Areas to Stay in Florence for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Florence. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the authentic Oltrarno district, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Florence, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the central San Lorenzo or the vibrant Santa Croce, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Florence that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Florence

Budget travellers visiting Florence can typically expect to spend between £50 and £80 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale.

Local meals in Florence are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore Florence extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Florence from the UK?

The winter months of January and February offer the best value for flights and hotels.

How much does a holiday to Florence cost from the UK?

Flights: £55–£160 return. Hotels: £75–£170 per night. City break packages: £280–£450

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Florence?

Florence Airport (FLR) is convenient, but many budget travellers fly into Pisa (PSA) due to lower fares.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Florence?

Booking 10 weeks in advance usually yields the best balance of price and choice.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

DIY booking is often preferred as it allows for flexibility in choosing flights from Pisa or Florence.
