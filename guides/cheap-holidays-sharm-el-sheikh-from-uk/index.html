Cheap Holidays To Sharm El Sheikh From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

For many UK holidaymakers, Sharm El Sheikh represents the perfect blend of exotic allure and wallet-friendly practicality. From the moment you arrive to see the vibrant Ras Mohammed National Park or wander around the bustling Naama Bay, you'll realize why this has become such a popular choice for those looking to maximize their travel budget. With frequent flights and diverse package options departing from regional UK airports, the journey to Sharm El Sheikh is both convenient and surprisingly economical.

What truly sets Sharm El Sheikh apart is the wealth of low-cost activities and dining options available. You can spend your days visiting the historic Saint Catherine's Monastery or simply soaking in the local atmosphere, knowing that your money goes much further here than in typical European capitals. By prioritizing world-class diving sites and desert adventures and opting for authentic local experiences, you can craft a memorable itinerary that respects your financial boundaries without sacrificing any of the magic.

Cheapest Time To Visit Sharm El Sheikh

January, February, and November are the cheapest months to find deals for Sharm El Sheikh.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Gatwick
  • London Stansted
  • Manchester
  • Birmingham
  • East Midlands

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £180–£380 return
  • Hotels: £40–£100 per night
  • All-inclusive packages: £450–£850

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Opt for all-inclusive to keep your daily spending to zero
  • Book diving and excursions through local reputable shops
  • Use resort shuttle buses to visit Naama Bay
  • Travel in January for the absolute lowest prices

Best Areas to Stay in Sharm El Sheikh for Budget Travellers

When it comes to finding affordable lodging in Sharm El Sheikh, location is everything. Savvy travellers often bypass the expensive city centres in favour of districts like the central Naama Bay, where you can find much better value for your money. These neighbourhoods usually boast excellent transport links, making it a breeze to commute to the main sights while enjoying a quieter, more local environment at night.

Areas such as the modern Nabq Bay and the authentic Old Market are particularly well-suited for those watching their pennies. Here, the concentration of budget-friendly hostels and simple apartments is higher, and the local dining scene reflects the more modest prices of the residents. Staying in these parts of Sharm El Sheikh allows you to stretch your budget further, giving you more freedom to spend on experiences and attractions rather than just a place to sleep.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Sharm El Sheikh

While your total spend in Sharm El Sheikh will vary based on your travel style, most budget-conscious visitors find that £30 and £50 a day is a realistic target. This budget allows for a clean and safe hostel bed, several local meals, and enough left over for public transport and one or two modest paid attractions. You can further optimize your spending by taking advantage of lunch specials and free museum days which are common in many parts of Sharm El Sheikh.

The cost of food and transport in Sharm El Sheikh is generally quite reasonable, particularly if you steer clear of the most tourist-heavy plazas. Local buses and trains offer a cost-effective way to get around, and street food vendors provide a delicious and cheap alternative to sit-down dining. By setting a daily limit and sticking to it, you can enjoy all the highlights of Sharm El Sheikh without any financial stress during or after your trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Sharm El Sheikh from the UK?

The winter months of January and February are the most budget-friendly, outside of the Christmas period.

How much does a holiday to Sharm El Sheikh cost from the UK?

Flights: £180–£380 return. Hotels: £40–£100 per night. All-inclusive packages: £450–£850

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Sharm El Sheikh?

Sharm El Sheikh International (SSH) is the main airport, served by TUI, EasyJet, and others.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Sharm El Sheikh?

Booking 3 months in advance is generally sufficient to secure a great all-inclusive deal.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Packages are almost always the cheapest way to visit Sharm El Sheikh due to the high cost of separate flights.
