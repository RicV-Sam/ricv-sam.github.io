Cheap Holidays To Sharm El Sheikh From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Sharm El Sheikh from the UK is the premier destination for affordable Red Sea luxury and world-class diving. Offering a unique blend of vibrant coral reefs and sun-baked desert landscapes, Sharm provides an exotic escape that delivers exceptional value. For UK travellers, the abundance of direct flights and competitive 2026 all-inclusive packages makes it a top-tier candidate for a winter sun break.

The secret to a cheap Sharm El Sheikh trip is mastering the all-inclusive model. Spend your days snorkeling in the Ras Mohammed National Park or exploring the historic Old Market for just the price of a local taxi. If you enjoy year-round sunshine, you might also like budget holidays to Tenerife. By utilizing resort shuttle buses and booking excursions through local reputable shops rather than hotel desks, you can experience the full magic of the Red Sea while keeping your holiday costs firmly under control. From the neon lights of Naama Bay to the tranquil bays of Nabq, Sharm offers a depth of adventure that is as accessible as it is unforgettable. Cheapest Time To Visit Sharm El Sheikh

January, February, and November are the cheapest months to find deals for Sharm El Sheikh.

  • London Stansted

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £190–£420 return from London or regional hubs
  • Hotels: £35–£85 per night for a well-rated 4-star resort
  • All-inclusive packages: £420–£780 for a 7-night stay

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Go All-Inclusive: Sharm is one of the few places where all-inclusive actually saves money. It covers your food, drinks, and snacks, allowing you to leave your wallet in the safe for most of the trip.
  • Shop at the Old Market: For souvenirs, spices, and authentic local food, skip the malls and head to the "Old Market" (Sharm al-Maya). It's much cheaper and offers a more traditional Egyptian experience.
  • Shore Snorkel for Free: Many hotels have their own house reefs. You don't need an expensive boat trip to see incredible marine life; just grab a mask and swim out from your resort jetty.
  • Book Excursions Locally: Avoid booking tours through your UK holiday rep. Reputable local shops in Naama Bay or the Old Market offer the same desert safaris and boat trips at much lower prices.

Best Areas to Stay in Sharm El Sheikh for Budget Travellers

To find the best value and avoid the highest prices of the ultra-luxury zones, consider these areas:

  • Naama Bay (Off-Beach): The city's main hub. Staying in a hotel just a 10-minute walk back from the beach can save you up to 40% while keeping you close to the action.
  • Old Town (Hadaba): Located on a cliff top, this area offers some of the most affordable guesthouses and older resorts with spectacular views and great access to the local market.
  • Shark's Bay: A smaller, more relaxed bay that often features excellent value boutique hotels and some of the best shore-snorkeling spots in Sharm.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Sharm El Sheikh

While your total spend in Sharm El Sheikh will vary based on your travel style, most budget-conscious visitors find that £30 and £50 a day is a realistic target. This budget allows for a clean and safe hostel bed, several local meals, and enough left over for public transport and one or two modest paid attractions. You can further optimize your spending by taking advantage of lunch specials and free museum days which are common in many parts of Sharm El Sheikh. The cost of food and transport in Sharm El Sheikh is generally quite reasonable, particularly if you steer clear of the most tourist-heavy plazas. Local buses and trains offer a cost-effective way to get around, and street food vendors provide a delicious and cheap alternative to sit-down dining. By setting a daily limit and sticking to it, you can enjoy all the highlights of Sharm El Sheikh without any financial stress during or after your trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Sharm El Sheikh from the UK?

The winter months of January and February are the most budget-friendly, outside of the Christmas period.

How much does a holiday to Sharm El Sheikh cost from the UK?

Flights: £180–£380 return. Hotels: £40–£100 per night. All-inclusive packages: £450–£850

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Sharm El Sheikh?

Sharm El Sheikh International (SSH) is the main airport, served by TUI, EasyJet, and others.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Sharm El Sheikh?

Booking 3 months in advance is generally sufficient to secure a great all-inclusive deal.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Packages are almost always the cheapest way to visit Sharm El Sheikh due to the high cost of separate flights.
