Cheap All Inclusive Holidays To Portugal From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Portugal has long been a standout destination for UK travellers seeking a high-quality escape without the premium price tag. Whether you are planning to explore the historic Belém Tower, visit the colourful Pena Palace, or discover the scenic Douro Valley, this location offers a remarkable balance of cultural depth and budget-friendly accessibility. Thanks to the high volume of low-cost carriers and competitive package deals available from major UK airports, reaching this part of the world is more affordable than ever before.

Beyond the initial travel costs, Portugal provides excellent value on the ground. From world-class street food and local markets to efficient public transport networks, budget travellers can enjoy a rich experience for a fraction of the cost of other major global hubs. If you're looking for sun and sea, you might also like budget travel in the Algarve. By choosing to travel during the shoulder seasons and focusing on melancholy Fado music and exceptional seafood, you can uncover the true essence of Portugal while keeping your holiday expenses firmly under control. Those interested in more island value should also see our guide to affordable Madeira.

Cheapest Time To Visit Portugal

The cheapest months to visit Portugal from the UK are:

  • November to March
  • April and May

The Algarve is a great winter destination for walking and golf, with very low all-inclusive rates during the off-season.

Best Areas to Stay in Portugal for Budget Travellers

Choosing the right neighbourhood is key to keeping your costs down in Portugal. For budget travellers, looking just outside the primary tourist hotspots can result in significant savings on accommodation. Areas that are well-connected by public transport hubs, such as the vibrant Lisbon, allow you to stay in more affordable guesthouses and hostels while still having easy access to all the major landmarks and attractions.

In Portugal, you will often find that neighbourhoods popular with students or locals offer a more authentic vibe alongside lower prices for both beds and meals. Consider exploring the historic Porto or the coastal Algarve, which frequently feature charming independent cafes, budget-friendly eateries, and a variety of low-cost lodging options ranging from boutique hostels to simple family-run hotels. By basing yourself in these vibrant districts, you not only save money but also get to experience a side of Portugal that many tourists miss.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Portugal

Budget travellers visiting Portugal can typically expect to spend between £50 and £80 per day depending on their choice of accommodation and daily activities. This range covers a comfortable stay in a budget-friendly hostel or guesthouse, a mix of supermarket snacks and local restaurant meals, and use of the efficient public transport system. Staying mindful of entry fees for major attractions and opting for free walking tours can help keep your daily spend at the lower end of this scale. For more all-inclusive inspiration, see our wider guide to all-inclusive deals.

Local meals in Portugal are usually very affordable, especially when eating in smaller family-run restaurants or exploring the diverse offerings at local food markets. Public transport is typically inexpensive and reliable, making it easy to navigate the city or region without the need for expensive taxis. By planning ahead and taking advantage of multi-day transport passes, you can explore Portugal extensively while ensuring your budget remains intact.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Portugal from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Portugal is typically during November to March and April and May, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Portugal cost from the UK?

Flights: £40–£130 return. All-inclusive packages: £400–£750. Coffee (if not all-in): £1

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Portugal?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Portugal?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Portugal holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Portugal due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
