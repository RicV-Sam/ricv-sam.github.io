Cheapest Time To Book Flights From The UK

1 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Finding the cheapest flights from the UK is not just about luck. Timing, seasonality and booking strategy can all have a major impact on the price you pay. If you're looking for where to fly, see our ranking of the cheapest holiday destinations from the UK.

Best Time To Book Flights

  • Short haul Europe: 6–8 weeks before travel
  • Long haul destinations: 8–16 weeks before travel
  • Peak holiday periods: Book 4–6 months early

Cheapest Days To Fly

  • Tuesday and Wednesday are often cheapest
  • Early morning or late night flights cost less
  • Avoid Friday evening and Sunday returns

Extra Budget Flight Tips

  • Use flight comparison websites to track prices.
  • Be flexible with your departure airport.
  • Consider "hidden city" ticketing or open-jaw flights for long-haul.
  • Sign up for airline newsletters for flash sale alerts.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the cheapest time to book flights from the UK?

Mid-week flights on Tuesdays and Wednesdays are typically the cheapest, especially in off-peak months like January and November.

How much do flights from the UK typically cost?

Short-haul flights can be as low as £30 return, while long-haul budget flights from the UK typically start around £400.

Which UK airports offer the cheapest international flights?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes for flights from the UK.

How many weeks in advance should I book flights from the UK?

For short-haul flights, book 6–8 weeks ahead. For long-haul, booking 5–6 months in advance often secures the best price.

Are return flights cheaper than booking two one-way tickets?

Not necessarily. With budget airlines, booking two one-way tickets can often be the same price or even cheaper than a return.
