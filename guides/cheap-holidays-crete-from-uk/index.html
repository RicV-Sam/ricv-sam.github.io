Cheap Holidays To Crete From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

For many UK holidaymakers, Crete represents the perfect blend of exotic allure and wallet-friendly practicality. From the moment you arrive to see the ancient Palace of Knossos or wander around the scenic Venetian harbour of Chania, you'll realize why this has become such a popular choice for those looking to maximize their travel budget. With frequent flights and diverse package options departing from regional UK airports, the journey to Crete is both convenient and surprisingly economical.

What truly sets Crete apart is the wealth of low-cost activities and dining options available. You can spend your days visiting the dramatic Samaria Gorge or simply soaking in the local atmosphere, knowing that your money goes much further here than in typical European capitals. If you are a fan of Greek history, you might also like budget travel in Rhodes. By prioritizing exceptional local cuisine and deep Minoan history and opting for authentic local experiences, you can craft a memorable itinerary that respects your financial boundaries without sacrificing any of the magic. Those looking for more island value should see our guide to cheap holidays in Cyprus or our Corfu budget guide.

Cheapest Time To Visit Crete

The cheapest months to visit Crete are May and October, during the shoulder season when the weather is still pleasant.

  • Hotels: £50–£120 per night

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Eat at local tavernas away from the main tourist strips
  • Rent a small car to explore hidden, free beaches
  • Travel outside of school holidays for the best deals
  • Buy local produce and wine from supermarkets

Best Areas to Stay in Crete for Budget Travellers

When it comes to finding affordable lodging in Crete, location is everything. Savvy travellers often bypass the expensive city centres in favour of districts like the historic Chania Old Town, where you can find much better value for your money. These neighbourhoods usually boast excellent transport links, making it a breeze to commute to the main sights while enjoying a quieter, more local environment at night.

Areas such as the lively Rethymno and the affordable Heraklion outskirts are particularly well-suited for those watching their pennies. Here, the concentration of budget-friendly hostels and simple apartments is higher, and the local dining scene reflects the more modest prices of the residents. Staying in these parts of Crete allows you to stretch your budget further, giving you more freedom to spend on experiences and attractions rather than just a place to sleep.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Crete

While your total spend in Crete will vary based on your travel style, most budget-conscious visitors find that £50 and £80 a day is a realistic target. This budget allows for a clean and safe hostel bed, several local meals, and enough left over for public transport and one or two modest paid attractions. You can further optimize your spending by taking advantage of lunch specials and free museum days which are common in many parts of Crete. For another top-value Greek destination, check out our budget guide to Zante.

The cost of food and transport in Crete is generally quite reasonable, particularly if you steer clear of the most tourist-heavy plazas. Local buses and trains offer a cost-effective way to get around, and street food vendors provide a delicious and cheap alternative to sit-down dining. By setting a daily limit and sticking to it, you can enjoy all the highlights of Crete without any financial stress during or after your trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Crete from the UK?

May and October offer the best combination of warm weather and low prices.

How much does a holiday to Crete cost from the UK?

Flights: £80–£190 return. Hotels: £50–£120 per night. All-inclusive packages: £350–£600

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Crete?

Heraklion (HER) and Chania (CHQ) are the two main gateways, both served by major UK low-cost carriers.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Crete?

Booking 3–4 months in advance is recommended for peak season, but shoulder season deals can be found later.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Packages often provide the best value for Crete, especially when they include transfers and meals.
