Cheap Holidays To Dubai From The UK | Budget Travel Guide

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Dubai is world-renowned for its superlative luxury, but beneath the shimmering skyscrapers lies a surprisingly accessible side to the city that budget-conscious UK travellers can enjoy. It's a place where you can experience the future of urban living and then step back into history in the traditional spice and gold souks for the price of a metro ticket.

The key to an affordable Dubai trip is balancing the glitz of the malls with the authentic charm of Old Dubai. By utilizing the ultra-modern Dubai Metro and eating in the diverse 'expat' neighbourhoods where incredible food from around the world is served at local prices, you can experience this desert metropolis without needing a millionaire's bank balance.

Cheapest Time To Visit Dubai

The cheapest months to visit Dubai from the UK are:

  • May
  • June
  • September

Visiting during these hotter months can result in significant savings on luxury hotels, though it is best to avoid the peak December period when prices are at their highest.

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £350–£650 return
  • Hotels: £80–£180 per night
  • Package holidays: £500–£900

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Use the Dubai Metro; it's clean, air-conditioned, and connects almost all the major tourist hubs for a very low cost.
  • Ride the 'Abra' (traditional wooden boat) across the Dubai Creek for just 1 AED (about 20p)—it's the best value experience in the city.
  • Eat in 'Old Dubai' (Deira and Bur Dubai) for incredible and cheap Indian, Pakistani, and Lebanese food.
  • Visit the public beaches like 'Kite Beach' or 'JBR Open Beach' which offer great facilities and views for free.

Best Areas to Stay in Dubai for Budget Travellers

Skip the expensive beach resorts and look for hotels in **Deira** or **Bur Dubai**. These are the historic hearts of the city, offering much better value for money and a more authentic atmosphere. **Al Barsha** is also a great mid-range option with excellent metro links to both the Marina and Downtown.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Dubai

A daily budget of **£70 to £120** is realistic. This allows for a good mid-range hotel, plenty of affordable dining in local eateries, and enough for one or two 'big' attractions like the Burj Khalifa or a desert safari. Many of Dubai's best experiences, like the Dubai Fountain show or the Abra boat rides, are free or very cheap.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Dubai from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Dubai is typically during May, June and September, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Dubai cost from the UK?

Flights: £350–£650 return. Hotels: £80–£180 per night. Package holidays: £500–£900

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Dubai?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Dubai?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Dubai holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Dubai due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
