Cheap Holidays To Bali From The UK

3 min read

Published by Budget Holidays Hub — UK travel research and budget holiday guides.

Bali from the UK is the ultimate long-haul value destination, where "budget" often feels like luxury. From the emerald rice terraces of Ubud to the world-class surf breaks of the Bukit Peninsula, the "Island of the Gods" offers a spiritual and tropical escape that is remarkably affordable once you arrive. For UK travellers, the key to a 2026 Bali trip is balancing the flight cost with the incredibly low daily expenses on the ground. Using one of the best travel booking platforms to track flight price drops is the best way to secure a long-haul bargain.

The secret to a cheap Bali trip is embracing the local way of life. Spend your days exploring ancient water temples or hiking to hidden waterfalls for just a few Pounds in entry fees. If you're looking for more Asian island inspiration, you might also like budget travel in Thailand. By staying in beautiful family-run guesthouses and dining at local "warungs," you can experience the authentic heart of Balinese culture while keeping your holiday costs firmly under control. Whether you're seeking a yoga retreat or a beachside adventure, Bali delivers world-class experiences for a fraction of the cost of European equivalents.

Cheapest Time To Visit Bali

The cheapest months to visit Bali from the UK are:

  • October and November
  • January to March

These months fall during the shoulder or rainy seasons, offering the lowest accommodation rates while still enjoying warm temperatures.

Cheapest Airports To Fly From The UK

  • London Heathrow
  • London Gatwick
  • Manchester

Typical Holiday Costs

  • Flights: £620–£880 return from major UK hubs (book well in advance)
  • Hotels: £15–£45 per night for a high-quality private guesthouse
  • Daily food & drink: £12–£28 for local dining and snacks

Budget Tips To Save Money

  • Use Grab or Gojek: These "super-apps" are essential for Bali. They offer the cheapest motorbike taxis, private cars, and food delivery at local prices, avoiding "tourist taxes."
  • Dine at Warungs: For the most authentic and affordable food, look for "Warung" in the name. You can get a huge plate of Nasi Campur or Mie Goreng for just £2-£3.
  • Rent a Scooter (With Care): At around £4-£6 per day, scooters are the most affordable way to explore. Ensure you have the correct license and insurance for Balinese roads.
  • Shop at Local Markets: Avoid the high-end boutiques. Visit markets like Art Sedana or local morning markets for fresh fruit and unique souvenirs at a fraction of the price.

Best Areas to Stay in Bali for Budget Travellers

Bali's neighborhoods vary wildly in price. For the best value, consider these hubs:

  • Ubud (Outer Villages): Stay just 10 minutes outside the center in villages like Penestanan for stunning rice paddy views and much cheaper guesthouses.
  • Canggu (Pererenan): While Canggu center is getting pricier, the neighboring Pererenan area offers a cooler, more local vibe and better-value boutique hostels.
  • Sanur: A more mature and relaxed coastal area that offers fantastic value for money on beachfront stays and authentic local dining compared to Seminyak.

Typical Daily Travel Costs in Bali

A very comfortable daily budget is **£30 to £55**. This covers a high-quality guesthouse or social hostel, several delicious meals at local warungs, scooter rental, and entry to various temples and waterfalls. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest time to visit Bali from the UK?

The cheapest time to visit Bali is typically during October and November and January to March, when flight and hotel prices are lower than the peak summer months.

How much does a holiday to Bali cost from the UK?

Flights: £650–£900 return. Hotels: £15–£40 per night. Daily food & drink: £10–£25

Which UK airports offer the cheapest flights to Bali?

London Gatwick, Stansted, Manchester, and Birmingham are common low-cost airports offering affordable routes to the area.

How far in advance should I book a cheap holiday to Bali?

Booking 6–10 weeks in advance typically offers the best balance of price and availability for Bali holidays.

Are package holidays cheaper than booking separately?

Package holidays often offer better value to Bali due to bulk pricing and consumer protection, though DIY booking can occasionally save money during sales.
